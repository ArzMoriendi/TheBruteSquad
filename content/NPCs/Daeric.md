### **Daeric Elmlight**

**Race:** Firbolg  
**Class:** Commoner  
**Occupation:** Merchant
**Location:** Longsaddle, North Faerûn (south of Mirabar)  
**Alignment:** Neutral Good

---

### **Background Overview:**

Daeric Elmlight is a soft-spoken, broad-shouldered Firbolg in his middle years, known throughout **Longsaddle** for his calm demeanor, honest trade, and the distinct golden mead produced from his family’s own apiary. Descended from a **druidic Firbolg clan** that once lived in the High Forest, Daeric chose a quieter, more practical life than that of his ancestors.

He has no formal druidic training, nor any ambition to pursue magic—but **he inherited just enough of the old ways** to keep food fresh, **calm an unruly horse**, or soothe a startled animal with whispered words and earthy herbs. These talents make him a skilled merchant and a reliable partner in the rugged frontier.

---

### **Family:**

- **Marta Elmlight (Wife)** – A stout, clever Firbolg woman with a shrewd mind for trade and inventory. She manages the books and barters better than most dwarves.
    
- **Tirna (17)** – Their eldest daughter, curious and energetic, who helps with the beekeeping and wants to someday travel to Silverymoon to learn herbcraft.
    
- **Orlem (14)** – A quiet boy, stronger than he looks, already hauling barrels and helping his father with honey pressing and wagon loading.
    
- **Bree (9)** – The youngest, often found singing to the bees or chatting to goats. Everyone in Longsaddle agrees: she’s got the “spark” that might bloom into true magic.
    

The Elmlights live in a **modest timber house** on the eastern edge of Longsaddle, with a small field of wildflowers, stacked hives, a grain barn, and a stone cellar for storing mead and trade goods. Their animals are well cared for, and many of their neighbors come to Daeric for advice on calming livestock or dealing with minor ailments.

---

### **Trade Connections:**

Daeric’s **signature product is Elmlight Gold Mead**, brewed from the honey of **docile, long-lived bees** raised by his family. The bees feed on enchanted wildflowers originally brought from the High Forest, and while the mead has no overt magic, it has a **warming effect** that dwarves and travelers swear “settles the bones.”

- **Trades With Mirabar**: Daeric barters casks of Elmlight mead to dwarven traders traveling the Long Road in exchange for **dwarven stone-cask mead**, known for its potency and mineral tang. This trade keeps him in high standing with dwarves, even if they find his pacifism odd.
    

---

### **Personality & Roleplaying Notes:**

- **Trust is earned**: He offers kindness and fair trade but watches closely before offering anything deeper.
    
- **Common sense over wisdom**: Not especially book-learned, but deeply experienced.
    
- **Wary of magic**: Beyond his minor gifts, he’s cautious around spellcasters—especially wizards (given Longsaddle’s Harpell residents).
    
- **Refuses to carry weapons**: Even on the road, he carries only a walking staff, preferring negotiation or retreat over violence.
    

---

### **Adventure Hook Possibilities:**

- **His bees start vanishing**, possibly tied to dark fey activity near the Evermoors.
    
- **A cask of Elmlight Gold Mead goes missing in transit to Mirabar**, causing tension with dwarves who accuse Daeric of breaking a standing agreement.
    
- One of his **children begins to show true druidic power**, attracting the interest of old forest spirits—or enemies of the Elmlight line.

## **Daeric Elmlight**

_Medium humanoid (Firbolg), neutral good_  
**Occupation:** Merchant, Beekeeper, Pacifist  
**CR:** 1/4 (50 XP)

---

### **Armor Class** 11 (natural wisdom & slow movements)

### **Hit Points** 11 (2d8 + 2)

### **Speed** 30 ft.

---

### **STR** 13 (+1)

### **DEX** 10 (+0)

### **CON** 12 (+1)

### **INT** 11 (+0)

### **WIS** 14 (+2)

### **CHA** 12 (+1)

---

### **Saving Throws** Wis +4

### **Skills** Animal Handling +4, Insight +4, Nature +3, Persuasion +3

### **Passive Perception** 14

### **Languages** Common, Elvish, Dwarvish, Sylvan

---

### **Firbolg Magic (1/day)**

Daeric can cast the following spells innately (spellcasting ability is **Wisdom**, save DC 12):

- **Detect Magic** (self only, concentration up to 10 minutes)
    
- **Disguise Self** (to appear more human-sized and nondescript)
    

---

### **Nature's Touch (3/day)**

Daeric can cast one of the following minor effects as a non-combatant magical ability:

- **Preserve Goods** – Touch up to 5 lbs of perishable goods (food, drink, herbs) to prevent spoilage for 24 hours.
    
- **Soothe Beast** – Touch an agitated or non-magical beast (CR 1/2 or lower) to calm it (as _animal friendship_, no save needed, lasts 1 minute).
    
- **Plant Whisper** – Encourage nearby plants to grow slightly over the next hour (cosmetic effect; used to enrich mead fields).
    

---

### **Pacifist’s Code**

Daeric **refuses to deal lethal damage**. He will never attack another creature except in defense of his family, and then only to distract or push back.

---

### **Actions**

**Walking Staff.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +2 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.  
_Hit:_ 3 (1d6) bludgeoning damage. Daeric uses this only to knock objects or defend against animals.

---

### **Reactions**

**Distracting Shout (1/short rest):** When a beast or humanoid targets an ally within 30 ft. with an attack, Daeric can shout or throw a noise-making item (like a clinking bottle or pouch of bees) to impose disadvantage on the attack.

---

### **Equipment**

- Cart of mead and goods
    
- Ledger and dwarven trade contracts
    
- Herbal poultices and bee salve
    
- Bee-smoker wand (harmless but can create a small 5 ft. smoke puff)
    
- A silver-edged wooden medallion bearing the Elmlight family sigil (an oak branch crossed with a honeycomb)
    

---

### **Roleplaying Daeric**

Daeric speaks softly, pauses before offering opinions, and watches the reactions of others before speaking further. He values honesty, loyalty, and nature—but he’s also a pragmatic businessman. He can serve as a quest-giver, local contact, supplier, or even the quiet hero behind the scenes.
