Perfect — here are **roleplay prompts for Geldryn Axebinder**, tailored to **multiple party states**: under arrest, cleared, or simply summoned. His tone varies depending on the party's situation and behavior but always reflects his duty, intelligence, and commitment to Mirabar's security and trade dominance.

These prompts include the **required line** integrated seamlessly into each version of the encounter.

---

## 🧔🏽‍♂️ **Geldryn Axebinder**

**Race**: Shield Dwarf  
**Role**: Councilor of the Hall of Sparkling Stones, Justiciar to the Marchion  
**Title**: Master of Trade Routes and External Threats  
**Personality**: Sharp-minded, calculating, polished. Wields words like weapons. Loyal to Mirabar's prosperity above all.  
**Voice**: Deep but smooth, with a formal dwarven cadence. More diplomat than warrior, but still intimidating.  
**Appearance**: Well-groomed beard bound in silver rings. Wears a crimson councilor’s mantle over dwarven mithral mail.

---

## 🧠 **Motivations and Background**

- Public image is everything — Geldryn fears **panic, disruption of trade, or political embarrassment**.
    
- Deeply **distrusts Goliaths**, especially Veldar's tribe (if known).
    
- Loyal to the Marchion but **acts independently** to preserve Mirabar’s standing.
    
- Sees use of **magical punishment collars** as regrettable but effective — a way to ensure “results without war.”
    

---

## 🎭 **Scenario 1: Party Is Under Arrest – Geldryn as Justiciar**

> _Tone: Stern, precise, skeptical but fair. He will not allow emotion to interfere with judgment._

---

**Introductory Prompt**:

> “You stand accused not by petty law, but by the deeper concern of security. You’ve entered our gates during a crisis, and your presence is... irregular. Especially you, Goliath. Your kind is not known for discretion. Or diplomacy.”

---

**Interrogation Prompts**:

- “Your name has weight, Veldar. Some of it earned, most of it soaked in blood. Why are you truly here?”
    
- “Speak now. Why should Mirabar trust you? Why should I?”
    
- “We lost five stonecutters to your kin near the Spine of the World. How do I know this isn’t the start of another raid under a different name?”
    

---

**Reveal of the Crisis**:

> _Said only after listening or under pressure, when the interrogation comes to a turning point._

> “One of our outposts — Hammerfall Run — went dark three days ago. The runners we sent didn’t return. The last message mentioned... shadows, large raiders, and some kind of dark sorcery. We need discretion. The Council can’t risk public panic or northern trade being disrupted.”

---

**If They Plead Their Case Well (Success)**:

> _Reluctant but impressed._

- “Perhaps I was too harsh. You don’t sound like raiders. You sound like survivors... and desperate ones, at that.”
    
- “Very well. You’ll go to Hammerfall Run — not as prisoners, but as agents of the Marchion. Bring us truth. And keep it quiet.”
    
- “I am trusting you with the silence of this city. If you break it, I will personally see you returned to stone.”
    

---

**If They Fail to Convince Him (Failure)**:

> _Cold and pragmatic._

- “The Marchion cannot gamble Mirabar’s safety on trust. But we will not waste potential. You’ll go to Hammerfall Run — with assurance.”
    
- “These collars are dwarven-forged. For every day you delay returning, they’ll tighten. And if you run? You won’t get far.”
    
- “Do your duty, and you’ll walk free. Fail — and the stone will mark your names as traitors.”
    

---

## 🎭 **Scenario 2: Party Is Not Arrested – Geldryn as Suspicious Councilor**

> _Tone: Diplomatic, probing, and reserved — but not hostile._

---

**Greeting Prompt**:

> “Welcome to the Hall of Sparkling Stones. I’ve read the gate report. Goliaths at our doors, a Furbolg in your company, and adventurers arriving in a time of crisis. Curious, to say the least.”

---

**Suspicion Prompt**:

- “You’ve wandered into Mirabar at a sensitive time. Many would call that luck. I call it timing.”
    
- “I’m told your Goliath companion has Ironclaw markings. Those tribes slaughtered miners in the west pass. Should we be concerned?”
    
- “Mirabar sees few Furbolgs. Fewer still walk our streets without reason. Why are you here?”
    

---

**Reveal of the Crisis (Unchanged Line)**:

> “One of our outposts — Hammerfall Run — went dark three days ago. The runners we sent didn’t return. The last message mentioned... shadows, large raiders, and some kind of dark sorcery. We need discretion. The Council can’t risk public panic or northern trade being disrupted.”

---

**Mission Offer**:

- “If your motives are true, prove it. Go to Hammerfall Run. Find out what happened. And report back directly to me — not the guards.”
    
- “This is not a request. The Marchion demands quiet results. Fail, and you’ll wish you'd been stopped at the gate.”
    
- “The Council will see your report. If we’re satisfied, you’ll be rewarded. If not... your presence in this city may be re-evaluated.”
    

---

## 🎭 **Scenario 3: Mid-Mission Debriefing (Optional Follow-up Use)**

> _Tone: Weary, more trusting, but watching for lies._

- “You returned. That means something. Now speak. What did you find in the ruins of Hammerfall Run?”
    
- “Dark sorcery confirmed, then. And raiders… Were they Goliath? Or something worse?”
    
- “The Marchion won’t like this. But he’ll believe me. And I’ll believe you — if you’ve brought truth, not tales.”
    

---

## 🪓 Bonus Lines for Dwarven Flavor

- “Mirabar’s stones don’t lie. But men? Men lie easy.”
    
- “Trade built this city. Blood protects it. I decide which you are: trade, or threat.”
    
- “You think the surface is harsh? Wait ‘til the stone judges you.”

# ⚖️ **Trial at the Hall of Sparking Stones**

_Scene: The party is seated or standing in the Council chamber — torches burn low, casting flickering shadows across marble pillars etched with dwarven runes. Geldryn Axebinder stands in ceremonial mithral mail, a hammer sigil on his chestplate and a golden rod of office in one hand._

> _He studies the party like a jeweler assessing flawed gems._

---

## 🎭 **Opening Address** (always spoken)

> **Geldryn (neutral tone):**  
> “You are not criminals. Yet. But you’ve entered Mirabar in dark times, and your presence stirs whispers. It falls to me, as Justiciar to the Marchion, to decide whether you are threat... or opportunity.”

---

## 🎲 **Persuasion Check Scene**

> _(Used if a party member earnestly tries to explain themselves, provide evidence, or appeal to justice.)_

### 💬 Dialogue Block

> **Geldryn (measured):**  
> “So... Speak, then. What brought you to Mirabar? What intent stirs beneath your cloaks and behind your words? Know this — I’ve heard thousands of lies. Don’t add yours to the list.”

---

### 🎯 **Persuasion DCs**

- **DC 15 – Partial Success:** He believes the party _isn’t actively hostile_, but doesn’t fully trust them.
    
    - _“You speak with the voice of reason... but not yet trust. You’ll prove your words in the dark, not here in the light.”_
        
- **DC 20 – Full Success:** He is convinced of their honesty.
    
    - _“Hm. Perhaps we are wrong to doubt. There’s steel in your eyes — not greed. Very well.”_
        
- **DC 25+ – Exceptional Success:** He respects them and offers extra support.
    
    - _“Few speak with such clarity under judgment. I’ll have rations and rune-maps prepared for Hammerfall Run.”_
        

---

## 🎲 **Deception Check Scene**

> _(Used if party lies or conceals the truth about their goals, past crimes, or Veldar’s background.)_

### 💬 Dialogue Block

> **Geldryn (suspicious):**  
> “Your story is smooth, but smooth stones are often the most treacherous. What are you hiding? What don’t you want Mirabar to see?”

---

### 🎯 **Deception DCs**

- **DC 15 – Partial Success:** He doubts them but lacks proof.
    
    - _“Hmm. I don’t like your tale, but I’ve no chain to bind you — not yet.”_
        
- **DC 20 – Full Success:** He buys the lie but marks them mentally.
    
    - _“Very well. You may go. But remember — I will be watching your return. Closely.”_
        
- **DC 25+ – Exceptional Success:** He believes them fully (even if the lie is wild).
    
    - _“Strange days, when such things are true. But your eyes hold no deceit. I trust you. For now.”_
        

> ⚠️ **Failed Deception (DC <15)**:  
> _“Enough. I’ve read too many false reports to miss the signs. You’re hiding something. So we’ll bind you in a truth that bites harder than words.”_

---

## 🎲 **Intimidation Check Scene**

> _(Used if the party attempts to strong-arm or threaten legal action, retaliation, or war if imprisoned.)_

### 💬 Dialogue Block

> **Geldryn (stone cold):**  
> “You think to threaten Mirabar? I’ve seen frost giants fall, and dragons kneel for coin. Words from vagabonds won’t sway my hand.”

---

### 🎯 **Intimidation DCs**

- **DC 18 – Partial Success:** He respects their strength but remains firm.
    
    - _“You’ve fire. Fine. Then prove it where it counts — in Hammerfall Run, not here in marble halls.”_
        
- **DC 23 – Full Success:** He backs down slightly and gives leeway.
    
    - _“I’d sooner see you useful than broken. Very well. The Run awaits.”_
        
- **DC 25+ – Exceptional Success:** He’s visibly unsettled or moved and will back them even against Council doubt.
    
    - *“I misjudged you. Perhaps your way _is_ what’s needed now. You’ll have my seal. Go.”
        

> ❌ **Failure (DC <18)**:  
> _“Bluster means nothing. I’ve sentenced kings with less cause. Speak again like that, and I’ll see your tongues sealed behind iron.”_

---

# 🔗 **Magical Collar Scene (Failure Case)**

> _After failed checks or if the party cannot prove innocence, Geldryn declares judgment._

---

**Geldryn (cold and formal):**

> “You may not wear shackles... but you’ll wear truth.”

> _He raises his rod of office. From the shadows, dwarven forge-priests approach, bearing four thick iron rings etched with glowing blue glyphs._

---

**As the Collars Are Placed** (for drama):

> - _“These are forged from underdeep mithral, quenched in spellfire, and bound by runes of loyalty.”_
>     
> - _“For each day you delay your return, the glyphs will tighten. For each lie you tell about what you find, they will burn.”_
>     
> - _“Flee, and your hearts will rupture like thunder eggs.”_
>     

> _The collars lock with a heavy **chkk-CLACK**, then glow faintly blue._

> _A subtle pressure pulses on their necks — not pain, but like a promise of it._

---

**Final Words Before the Mission**:

> “One of our outposts — Hammerfall Run — went dark three days ago. The runners we sent didn’t return. The last message mentioned... shadows, large raiders, and some kind of dark sorcery. We need discretion. The Council can’t risk public panic or northern trade being disrupted.”  
> “You’ve got ten days. Don’t make me regret giving you even one.”
