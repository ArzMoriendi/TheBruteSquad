### Scene Setter
 

**CHES 24**  
When last we left our adventurers... 

The Brute Squad stood victorious within the ritual chamber deep beneath [[DM_Hammerfall Run]], where shattered goliath zombies and the frozen remains of their enemies marked the cost of battle. They had broken the will of a necromantic mage, survived deadly magic, and claimed a strange hovering relic of stone and ruby that pulsed with ancient power. With no safer refuge to be found, the party rested within the chamber itself. However, sleep brought no peace. Veldar felt the strengthening presence of the Beast once more. Zwargie was praised and empowered by the dark voice that continued to guide him. GhoulMan was tormented by visions of his murdered family and awoke with vengeance burning hotter than ever. Only Leyawiin rose uncertain, no longer convinced that the path of the Bladesinger was truly hers.

Leaving Hammerfall Run behind, the party emerged to find the mountain in the midst of a tempestuous blizzard. Their first attempt to descend was driven back by wind and ice, forcing them to wait until the storm relented. Even then, the mountain was not finished with them. A sudden partial avalanche battered Leyawiin, Veldar, and Bronn before the group could regroup and press onward. At last, through thinning snow and dawn light, the great city of [[DM_Mirabar]] appeared ahead. Escorted inside by Captain [[DM_Durnan Kraghelm]], our adventurers were brought once more before Justiciar [[DM_Geldryn Axebinder]], where their deeds were confirmed and Veldar was fully cleared of all suspicion.

When the party presented the relic, Geldryn revealed its true identity as the legendary Stoneheart Reliquary, a dwarven artifact capable of transforming common ore into its purest form. Understanding the importance of what they had recovered, the Brute Squad entrusted it to Mirabar’s care and were richly rewarded with gold, a Bag of Holding, honored status within the city, access to its libraries, and future use of its portal chamber. In the halls of learning, Leyawiin found clarity at last under the stern guidance of [[DM_Harbek Bronzewarden]], abandoning Bladesinging to pursue the path of Evocation. With gear repaired, supplies gathered, and warm beds finally waiting, the Brute Squad rested in comfort as proven heroes of Mirabar. With roads, seas, and portals now open before them, one question remained: Where would they go next?

**CHES 24**  

From the end of the battle with the mage and the goliath, Bronn wakes up enlightened and more flexible, blessed by [[DM_Kelemvor]].  

While Leyawiin studied, Bronn joined the shopping trip, purchasing a small mirror (2 silver)
He also etched a sunburst into a copper piece. 

In the tavern of the inn that [[DM_Daeric Elmlight|Daeric]] has led our party too,  they discuss what to do next.  The party debates what they should do with the heads they have been carrying around (the satyr, [[DM_Kragnoroth the Drunk|Kragnoroth]], and [[DM_Kragnoroth the Drunk|Vanessa]]). Zwarg brings up the [[DM_Session 08|1500gp bounty]]
. Bronn and Sarhice bring up ideas of offloading the heads quickly.  Leyawiin suggests going to Luskan directly and claiming the bounty.

They head back to the Hall of Sparkling Stones, and talk to the Justiciar about the bounty, revealing to the head of justice of Mirabar that they have been carrying around the heads of three creatures in their leather sacks.  After a few questions back and forth, they learn there is no "formal" bounty through alliance channels. They go so far as to ask about a "seedy underbelly" to potentially collect the bounty from someone who deals in criminal trades. The Justiciar shooed them out. 

The party initially decided to go to [[DM_Luskan]] and looked into a wagon or horses.  They got directions to the Northwall Livery and spoke to the stablemaster about horses and wagons. They looked at mules, draft horses, and riding horses.  Zwarg looked at a war horse who he fondly called "Tink Tink"

While Veldar, Zwarg, Sarhice, and Leyawiin discuss horses and wagons, Bronn and GhoulMan ask about dire rams.  The stable master said to check the dwarven part of Mirabar, one layer underground.  Initially, he went without a direction. Eventually, he found the main thoroughfare to the dwarven underground of Mirabar.  

With the help of a guard, Bronn and GhoulMan eventually find Ironwool Stables, where they witness a dwarven woman throwing around rams.  They start talking and haggling price, eventually coming down to 140 gp for ram with saddle, but the cleric must try to heal the stable woman's mother.  Bronn learns the the stable woman's name is Dagna.  She escorts him to the back of a humble house, and the smell is overpowering.  Death, puss, and disease.  The mother is wrapped in bandages with spots of sores, wounds, and other fluids.  Dagna shares she has been to every healer and holy person in the city, including the temple of [[Additional Deities|Moradrin]], but no one has been able to heal her mother.  Bronn attempts to use a scroll of healing followed by prayer of healing. While her visible wounds healed and her breathing eased, she still remained sickly and incapacitated. Ultimately, he was unable to determine what was the cause of the illness. 

Dagna thanked him for his attempt and, recognizing his work and sacrifice, she agreed to give Bronn a ram and saddle for 140gp and a favor to be used later.  She gave him one of the oldest work goats, an aged but spry goat the Dagna calls Geezer.  

Bronn rides Geezer back to the stables, with GhoulMan trailing and learns the rest of the party decided the cost to buy a wagon and steed were too much and headed back to the inn and tavern they were staying at. When Bronn joined up with them, the decided to ask Daeric if he had a black market contact.  Daeric leaves for a while and eventually comes back.  He asks them to a back room. 

They enter and see a humanoid with brown/taupe skin, cracks across like baked mud or clay, and gold eyes.   Dust and dirt rose from him as he moved.  They learned his name was [[DM_Mirabar|Marmoreal]].  He spoke with an even tone and told them he helps Daeric find things on the black market. They told Marmoreal about the heads of Kragnoroth and the bounty out of Luskan. 

He told them to wait here and he returned with another person that resembled a dwarf with pale, ashen-gray skin, overly white sclera, a bald head with quills along the back of his head.  Marmoreal introduced him as [[DM_Mirabar|Theriggs]], a fence and validator for bounties. 

The party showed him the head of Kragnoroth, skin sloughing off and eyeball hanging. Thriggs verified that the captain of [[DM_Luskan|Ship Suljack]] had a bounty out.  He said in the current condition he wasn't sure he could verify this was Kragnoroth and offered 125gp.  The party agreed. They offered him the other two heads, and he declined saying there was no value. 

They completed their business, and went to sleep.  

**CHES 25**

The next day, they left the tavern/inn, and sought out [[DM_Mirabar|Ghorza]] at the Greystone Smith. Along the way, they disposed of the other heads.  Veldar attempted to deceive Ghorza that he already paid, but she was not so easily swayed.  He paid the 22gp and they made their way back to the Hall of Sparkling Stones. 

They came back to the Justiciar and said they were ready to travel to Neverwinter.  Bronn mentioned Geezer, and Geldryn said they would have to use the Harper's portal as the one in the Hall was too small.  He provided a letter of certification.  They traveled to it, and were given access to the larger portal.  They all traveled through (including Geezer) and ended up coming through a portal in a Harper's station outside of [[DM_Neverwinter]] proper.   More adventure awaits!
