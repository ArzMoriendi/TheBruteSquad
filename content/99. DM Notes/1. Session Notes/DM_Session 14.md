### Scene Setter
 
**CHES 21**

Our adventurers turn their attention to the mine at Hammerfall Run.  They make their way into the tunnel, following the map given to them by Durni (the site foreman).  Traveling down the stone mineshaft, Zwarg triggers a trap taking damage from darts flying from the wall. Learning from this, the party uses Leyawiin and some light magic to detect and find the rest.  As they descend into the mine, they look for any signs of either the camp miners and residents or the potential attackers. 

As the party approached the primary chamber, they detected a glyph of warding.  Zwarg sought to set it off by lobbing a weapon at it.  He overestimated his strength and caused a partial cave-in between the hit and the explosion.  The party had to dig out to clear the pathway.

The Brute Squad finds their way into the primary chamber where the other pathways branch off.  They chose the left pathway, traveling to the wall that Durni identified and said the mining company stopped.  Carefully, the party made their way to the doorway.  They were greeted by a very large, well finished, granite-like doorway/wall. In bas relief, a large dwarven figure stares out before them, armored and solemn.  He holds large scales, one plate before them, in a large alcove of the wall, the other disappearing into the wall. 

The party attempts to put items (rocks and stones) onto the scales, and nothing moves it. 

After a successful investigation check, the party sees ancient Dwarven runes.  Bronn reads aloud:

“Not gold nor gem gilded crown  
Shall turn the lock or win renown

By blood freely given and hammer’s cry,  
By sweat that falls and will that won’t die

By calloused hand and steadfast soul,  
The stone shall yield, the gates unroll.

Let trinkets gleam for softer kin -  
Here, only toil shall grant you in.”

The party pondered this, with Zwarg adding blood from one of the darts he took from the trap and Sarhice adding a broken hammer head he picked up in the camp.  This, mixed with sweat from moving the rocks, caused the scale plate to drop, triggering the door to open.

The party went in and took in a large and ancient, Dwarven burial chamber.  Lined with several dozens to over a hundred sarcophagi, the air was still and dank.  Bronn felt the solemnity of the location and warned his peers.  No undead were sensed, but magic was detected near the center of chamber

The party made their way back to the primary chamber and went down the right-hand passage, going towards where Durni had said the current work was being done.  They find their way to the main branching pathway, discovering that there was a mass cave-in and all the pathways are currently blocked.  Thanks to a very lucky investigation roll, the party manages to find and harvest a fair amount of silver from nearby veins, giving the silver to Bronn.

The Brute squad made their way back to the burial chamber, where Bronn casts _Ceremony_, dispelling the magic ward in the area and allowing the buried dwarves to rest in perpetuity.  Finding the area safe, the party camped by the front door (full rest), and then left to explore further into the cavern.

After their rest, the party made its way to the primary chamber, going through a recently broken through cavern and discovering another larger, metallic and stone wrought doorway with three interlocking hexes.  Investigation and perception shows one panel damaged by physical attacks, bearing scratches, dents, and pock marks, and one panel with scorch marks, lightning, etching, and other arcane marks.  The third panel bears very minimal marks, but shows images of crowns, gems, and jewels.

After further investigation, the party finds another poem in Dwarven runes. Bronn translates:

“Three Stones set in union stand,  
Not twins, nor thrice of singe hand 

One was born for ringing steel.  
One was shaped for rune and zeal.  
One was carved for honor’s face-  
Strike it not, nor mar its grace.

Forge and spell in one same breath-  
As anvil’s spark and bellow’s death.

Divide the beat and bar the way.  
Strike as one-or none shall pass this day.”

Again, after discussion, they decide to try different methods.  The party eventually/ultimately uses a physical attack on the panel that was dented (Veldar with hammer?) and a magical attack on the panel that was scorched (Leyawiin with fire bolt?) and a third holding up something of value to the third panel (Sarhice & coin?).

The party had to time their attack and it was tricky to get the timing right (a couple failed shots), but they eventually got it and the doors open.

The session ended with the party waiting to enter the chamber beyond.
