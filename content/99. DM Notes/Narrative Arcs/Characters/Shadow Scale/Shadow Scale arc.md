Perfect — that’s a really nice pivot for Shadow Scale! Him already having undergone a **Ritual of Realignment** to Asgoroth and shifting to **Neutral** changes the texture of each arc in an elegant way. Instead of being about “whether he will abandon Bahamut,” the story now becomes:

➡️ **Bahamut’s faithful see him as an apostate.**  
➡️ **Elven kin question whether he is still Elarael.**  
➡️ **Tiamat’s cult sees him as ripe for corruption, given his neutrality.**

This makes Shadow Scale theologically and morally “in-between,” which is a great fit for Asgoroth (the primordial dragon of balance/creation/destruction). It also deepens the arcs without requiring rewrites of the overall structure.

Here’s how the update shifts things:

---

## **Level 5–8 Arc: Branded Heretic**

- **Change from Previous Version:** Bahamut’s priests don’t just see him as “a dragonborn who doesn’t worship Bahamut” — now they know he has **chosen Asgoroth**, a cosmic rival of Bahamut. That makes him worse than an unbeliever: he’s a traitor.
    
- **How This Plays:**
    
    - Priests in Port Nyanzaru (or Neverwinter) start cordial, but upon realizing his allegiance, quietly mark him as an **apostate**.
        
    - They put out a **heretic’s contract** on him — not just to kill, but to “purify” (abduction + forced repentance).
        
    - Assassins/mercenaries now sometimes shout or bear holy marks of Bahamut, reinforcing that this is religious persecution, not just random hits.
        
- **Reward:** Instead of a Bahamut “blessing,” the narrative reward is **resolve**: Shadow Scale learns that even neutrality is not safe from judgment. You can still give a boon, e.g.:
    
    - **Homebrew Boon – Balance of the First Dragon:** Once per long rest, he can add **+1d4 to any attack or saving throw** if he rolls exactly the same number on another d20 that turn (reflecting Asgoroth’s symmetry/duality).
        

---

## **Level 9–13 Arc: The Choice of Flesh**

- **Change from Previous Version:** His neutrality makes the **elf vs. dragonborn dilemma sharper.**
    
    - If he chooses to become an elf again, he reverts to Elarael, losing Drakewarden & drake — and there’s no guarantee Asgoroth will continue to favor him in that form. His “cosmic neutrality” risks collapsing into simply “another mortal ranger.”
        
    - If he remains dragonborn, he is doubling down on his **new identity as Shadow Scale**, rejecting his past entirely. That makes him a permanent outsider to elves _and_ Bahamut’s faithful, but strengthens his role as Asgoroth’s odd champion.
        
- **Optional Addition:** You could tie Asgoroth into this choice: the ritual circle whispers — “One flesh is harmony, one flesh is war. Which are you?”
    
- **Reward Update:**
    
    - **Elf path:** Instead of an elven feat, maybe grant him an **Ancestral Legacy** (TCoE custom lineage rules) — a permanent feature like _Darkvision_ or _Keen Senses_. But he loses Drakewarden forever.
        
    - **Dragonborn path:** He receives an Asgoroth-themed weapon or charm — e.g. **Twin-Soul Relic (Homebrew Wondrous Item):** Once per day, he can switch the damage type of his drake’s attack between radiant and necrotic.
        

---

## **Level 14–18 Arc: Tiamat’s Chains**

- **Change from Previous Version:** Neutrality makes Shadow Scale an even _better_ target for Tiamat’s cult. They see him as proof that Bahamut rejected him and that Asgoroth doesn’t care enough to keep him. They frame themselves as his **true destiny.**
    
- **How This Plays:**
    
    - If Dragonborn: The cult declares him “the Twilight Dragon” and tempts him to “step into his true role.” The offer feels _plausible_ because he’s not Good anymore.
        
    - If Elf: They kidnap him and forcibly try to **re-dragonborn** him in Tiamat’s image, completing the “cycle of betrayal” — elf ➝ Bahamut’s dragonborn ➝ Asgoroth’s neutral dragonborn ➝ Tiamat’s dragonborn.
        
- **Reward Update:**
    
    - If he resists: He gains a **Draconic Gift** (FToD, p. 28) tied to _Asgoroth’s Balance_ — e.g. _Draconic Senses_.
        
    - If he accepts but seeks redemption: He must undergo another dangerous **Ritual of Realignment** at a temple of Bahamut or Asgoroth to purge the corruption. Doing so may scar him permanently (e.g. mechanical boon but visual mark of corruption).
        

---

### **Narrative Throughline With Update**

- **Early Tier:** Bahamut’s faithful brand him heretic.
    
- **Mid Tier:** His elven kin ask if he wants to reclaim Elarael or remain Shadow Scale.
    
- **High Tier:** Tiamat’s cult tries to claim him as their prophesied Twilight Dragon.
    
- **Core Theme:** Neutrality is not “safe” — it means _everyone_ tries to claim or condemn him, but in the end, Shadow Scale must decide who he is.
