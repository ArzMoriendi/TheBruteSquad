# Side Arc: **Ashes of the Pale Flame**

---

## **Overview:**

Sarhice, an elf resurrected as a Hollow One by the enigmatic entity known as **The Pale Flame**, struggles with his unnatural existence. Torn between life and undeath, he seeks to reclaim his soul, break his pact, and either return to true life or find peace in death. The Pale Flame’s influence subtly undermines the natural cycle of life and death, setting Sarhice on a path fraught with moral and spiritual tension.

---

## **Adventure Hook:**

Sarhice’s ghostly half becomes more restless, manifesting emotions and whispers that hint at a fractured soul. His companions sense his internal struggle, and as strange phenomena occur around him (missing souls, disrupted rites), they must decide whether to help Sarhice break free or allow him to remain bound to the Pale Flame.

---

## **Phase 1: Echoes and Doubts (Levels 8–10)**

### Objectives:

* Sarhice begins to experience intrusive visions and ghostly whispers from his lost spirit.
* He receives mysterious tasks from the Pale Flame that challenge natural laws (e.g., stealing funerary objects, capturing last breaths).
* Party members Bronn (cleric of Kelemvor) and Zwargie (paladin of Lathander) detect spiritual disturbances and confront Sarhice about his pact.

### Key Events:

* Discovery of the silver coin with arcane symbols linked to his resurrection.
* Sarhice’s ghost half occasionally influences his emotions and actions.
* Growing ideological tension between Sarhice and his allies over his methods and patron’s goals.

### Thematic Questions:

* What defines the soul?
* Is the boundary between life and death inviolable?

---

## **Phase 2: Breaking the Threshold (Levels 11–16)**

### Objectives:

* Sarhice uncovers the truth: his soul was split during resurrection.
* He embarks on a quest to retrieve memory fragments and denied rites to reunite his spirit.
* Rituals require assistance from party members tied to divine forces (Bronn and Zwargie).
* Journey to the Pale Flame’s Threshold — an ethereal gateway between Shadowfell and the mortal plane.

### Key Events:

* Recover lost memory shards from the site of his death.
* Conduct rites interrupted at his original deathsite.
* Encounter other Hollow Ones or spirits trapped between life and death.
* Face temptations and revelations about the nature of his pact.

### Thematic Questions:

* Can death be undone without corrupting natural order?
* What is the cost of reclaiming a fractured soul?

---

## **Phase 3: Choice of Fates (Levels 17–20)**

### Objectives:

* As the party confronts the climax of *Tomb of Annihilation* (destruction of the Atropal/Soulmonger), Sarhice’s soul becomes whole again.
* Sarhice must make a profound choice to break or accept the Pale Flame’s pact.
* Possible outcomes manifest, each with significant narrative and mechanical consequences.

### Key Events:

* Final confrontation with the Pale Flame’s influence.
* Ritual or battle to sever or redefine the pact.
* Potential intervention from divine agents of Lathander or Kelemvor.
* Decision-making moment determining Sarhice’s fate.

### Thematic Questions:

* What does it truly mean to live or die?
* How far will Sarhice go for freedom or power?

# 1) What Sarhice is Collecting for The Pale Flame

The key is that what Sarhice gathers must:

- Not look like outright desecration (so no “dig up the body and steal the soul”).
    
- Create subtle friction with **Bronn (Kelemvor)** and **Zwargie (Lathander)**.
    
- Be things the Pale Flame can twist toward his purpose (eroding the line between life/death).
    

Here’s the finalized list:

---

### **Sarhice’s Collections (Core Loop)**

1. **Last Whispers (Final Words/Secrets)**
    
    - Sarhice can capture the final words, secrets, or confessions of the dying.
        
    - Mechanic: When a creature dies within 30 ft, Sarhice can use his reaction to make a **Charisma (Persuasion or Intimidation) check** vs. DC 15. On a success, he records their final “whisper” into his Gravekindle.
        
    - Why it matters: For Kelemvor, this interferes with the natural flow of judgment. For Lathander, it twists sacred moments of mercy into currency.
        

---

2. **Shards of Memory (Mementos of the Dead)**
    
    - Instead of stealing souls outright, Sarhice is tasked with recovering **objects linked to the dead** — bloodied tokens, journals, burial trinkets.
        
    - Mechanic: If Bronn performs _Gentle Repose_ (PHB), Sarhice can contest with an **Arcana check vs. Bronn’s Spell Save DC** to subvert it. On success, the object becomes imbued with an echo of memory instead of fully sanctified.
        
    - Why it matters: This doesn’t “raise undead” but prevents full consecration. The Pale Flame feeds on these unmoored echoes.
        

---

3. **Unfinished Dawnlight (Fragments of Lathander’s Radiance)**
    
    - Sarhice can capture **traces of dawnlight** during sunrise or consecrated funerary rites.
        
    - Mechanic: Requires a special vessel (homebrew magic item: _Lantern of Hollow Dawn_) that can hold this energy. Once per long rest, during a sunrise or ritual, Sarhice can make a **DC 16 Arcana check** to siphon 1 charge of “Dawnlight Fragment.”
        
    - Why it matters: This is subtle blasphemy — Lathander’s light stolen to empower the Pale Flame. Zwargie would be torn if he found out.
        

---

### Why This Works

- **Subtle tension**: Sarhice isn’t raising zombies or stabbing priests — he’s quietly collecting memories, whispers, and fragments that the others might not even notice at first.
    
- **Cleric/Paladin conflict**: Bronn and Zwargie _would feel_ something wrong with funerary rites or dawn rituals but not always trace it to Sarhice immediately.
    
- **Narrative room**: Each collection feels like a “small side-task” layered into _ToA_’s bigger dungeon crawls and city intrigue.

# 2) Strongest 3 Options for The Pale Flame’s Endgame

The Pale Flame should be a **subversive manipulator of metaphysics**, not just another death god. Here are the three sharpest options:

---

### **A. The Third Path (The Gray Domain)**

_Goal:_ Establish a new metaphysical domain “between” life and death — where Hollow Ones and echoes dwell.  
_Consequence:_ Souls begin to “slip” into the Pale Flame’s realm instead of going to their rightful afterlife.  
_Tie to ToA:_ The Soulmonger is already tampering with souls; Pale Flame uses its destruction as proof that “the rules can be rewritten.”

---

### **B. Soul Mosaic**

_Goal:_ Craft a “new soul” by stitching together fragments — last whispers, memories, stolen dawnlight.  
_Consequence:_ If successful, the Pale Flame births a **soulless-but-sentient entity** outside all divine jurisdiction.  
_Tie to ToA:_ Could parallel the Atropal — a failed soul experiment — giving Sarhice an eerie mirror of what _not_ to become.

---

### **C. The Hollow Dawn**

_Goal:_ Rewrite the meaning of dawn itself. Instead of renewal, dawn becomes liminal — a time when the living and dead coexist.  
_Consequence:_ If achieved, Lathander’s core symbolism is undermined; death becomes as cyclical as sunrise.  
_Tie to ToA:_ The jungles of Chult are filled with deathless curses. A dawn corrupted to hold the dead in place fits perfectly with the setting.

---

# 3) Steps for Sarhice to Become **Unbound**

Breaking free from The Pale Flame must be **epic-tier**, requiring intervention from gods or cosmic forces. Here’s the progression:

---

### **Step 1: Realization (Level 8–12)**

- Sarhice learns his soul is split — his ghost is the missing fragment.
    
- He discovers the **Gravekindle is the anchor** binding him to the Pale Flame.
    

---

### **Step 2: Memory Restoration (Level 12–15)**

- Collect **memory shards** of his death — scattered whispers, echoes, rites denied. 
    
- Mechanics: Each shard can be bound to the ghost with a _Ceremony_ spell (XGtE, Bronn’s domain).
    
- Tension: Bronn must help with rites, Zwargie with dawnlight — both uneasy aiding a warlock’s patron struggle.
    

---

### **Step 3: Quenching the Flame (Level 15+)**

- Sarhice must **extinguish the Gravekindle**, but this can only happen at a threshold between life and death.
    

**Methods:**

1. **Soulmonger’s Collapse** (_ToA climax_)
    
    - When the Atropal is destroyed, Sarhice can use a _Wish_ (PHB) or _Divine Intervention_ (Cleric, PHB) to direct the released souls into himself, reuniting with his ghost.
        
2. **Godly Intervention**
    
    - Bronn petitions Kelemvor (_Divine Intervention_, PHB).
        
    - Zwargie petitions Lathander (Homebrew: “Blessing of the Dawn,” requires _Ceremony_ at sunrise).
        
    - The gods don’t interfere directly but offer the _rite_ to sever the Gravekindle.
        
3. **The Ghost as Patron** (Homebrew Pact Shift)
    
    - Sarhice’s ghost becomes strong enough to offer a pact — replacing the Pale Flame.
        
    - Mechanic: Swap Hexblade for _Archfey_ (memory + emotion patron) or a homebrew **“Soulbound” Patron** with ghost-flavored invocations.
        

---

### **Possible Outcomes**

1. **Rebirth** – Fully alive again, regains druid levels instead of warlock.
    
2. **Release** – Ghost and body reunite only to pass on; Sarhice dies peacefully.
    
3. **Transcendence** – Ghost and Hollow merge into a new liminal being (new subclass features).
    
4. **Remain Bound** – Pale Flame offers more power; Sarhice chooses to stay.
    
5. **Ghost Patron** – Sarhice becomes a Warlock of his own soul. His ghost now _is_ the threshold he serves.
