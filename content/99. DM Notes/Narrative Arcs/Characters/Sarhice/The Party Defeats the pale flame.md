Below are **concise, quest-shaped ideas**, grouped by the paths you’re considering, each fitting *He Who Waits at the Threshold* as a Shadowfell usurper-god.

---

## I. Enlisting the Gods: *“Prove This Soul Is Not His”*

### **Kelemvor – The Audit of the Dead**
Kelemvor agrees to judge Sarhice’s case, but demands the party expose **other stolen crossings** tied to the Pale Flame—souls prevented from reaching their rightful end. The quest involves dismantling a Shadowfell conduit (or cult) feeding the Door, restoring dozens of souls to the Fugue Plane. If successful, Kelemvor formally *reclaims jurisdiction* over Sarhice: he may return to full life by decree, or be guaranteed an undisturbed natural death when his time comes.

---

### **Lathander – A Dawn That Should Not Exist**
Lathander offers life, but only if Sarhice proves he is more than a stolen spark. The party must carry a fragment of dawnlight into the Shadowfell or Chult’s darkest ruin and **ignite it where resurrection went wrong**, undoing a Pale Flame corruption. If done, Sarhice is either fully reborn as a living being—burning away Hollow One traits—or the light burns out cleanly, leaving him mortal and finally *able* to die.

---

### **Nature Deity (Silvanus / Ubtao Echo) – Transformation, Not Restoration**
A nature god refuses the false binary of life vs. death and offers **return to the cycle** instead. The party must end a region where death stagnates (undeath, soul-binding, shadow blight) and let decay finish its work. Sarhice’s fate becomes one of surrender: he either re-enters life as something wholly natural again, or fades peacefully into death, reclaimed as compost for the world—utterly unreachable by the Pale Flame.

---

## II. Negotiating with the Pale Flame: *“What Is a Door Will Always Want Passage”*

### **The Offer – A Threshold Greater Than a Soul**
The Pale Flame is not sentimental—it is ambitious. It offers Sarhice’s release if the party helps it **claim a god-level threshold**: a divine transition point such as the moment of apotheosis, ascension, or mass death narrowly averted. The party can accept, twist the deal (redefine the “threshold”), or set it up to fail—forcing the Pale Flame to honor the agreement while gaining nothing lasting.

---

### **The Counter‑Offer – Close One Door to Open Another**
The Pale Flame may accept Sarhice’s freedom if the party agrees to **carry a new threshold elsewhere**—transporting the Door’s influence to a collapsing demiplane, dead god’s husk, or sealed planar scar. Sarhice is freed because the patron no longer needs *him*, only what he represented. This sets up future consequences without invalidating the PCs’ victory.

---

## III. Destroying the Door: *“If It Is Burned, It Becomes Only Stone”*

### **Douse the Pale Flame**
In the Shadowfell, the party discovers the flame is sustained by unresolved deaths and unfinished goodbyes. To douse it, they must **resolve Sarhice’s own unfinished business**—return a relic, forgive an enemy, or be forgiven. Once extinguished, the Door becomes inert, and Sarhice’s fate resolves naturally: he wakes alive and mortal—or gently begins to weaken, finally allowed to die.

---

### **Break the Threshold Stone**
The Door is ancient but not invulnerable. The party learns the threshold stone is fused from multiple stolen deaths—breaking it releases them all at once. Sarhice survives the ritual only if another power claims him; otherwise, he dies peacefully shortly after, smiling as the Door collapses behind him, useless forever.

---

## IV. Another Plane’s Help: *“Not All Oppositions Are Holy”*

### **An Archfey – Rewrite the Metaphor**
An archfey offers to help—not by fighting the Pale Flame, but by **changing what Sarhice is**. Through a perilous bargain, Sarhice ceases to be “a being who stands at a threshold” and becomes something undefined by death at all. The Door loses its anchor. Sarhice lives again… or fades away… but only after one wonderfully strange, meaningful season.

---

### **A Demon Lord – Destroy the Concept**
A demon lord doesn’t care about balance, only denial. In exchange for a future favor, they offer a weapon or ritual that **destroys the idea of a threshold**—turning doors into walls, transitions into ends. It works. Sarhice is freed absolutely—but the party knows they have empowered something that *hates endings*, and that bill will come due.
