### **False Hydra in D&D 5e**

The **False Hydra** is a **fan-made**, **homebrew** horror monster that has become a **legend** in the D&D community — especially in darker, psychological or horror-themed campaigns. It’s not an official creature in *any* published 5e sourcebooks, but it’s widely used by DMs looking to add **existential dread** and **mind-bending fear** to their games.

---

## 🧠 False Hydra – Overview

* **Type**: Aberration (or Monstrosity, depending on version)
* **CR**: Varies (typically between CR 5–10, depending on number of heads and regional influence)
* **Alignment**: Usually Neutral Evil
* **Origin**: Created by a fan named *Goblin Punch*, later adapted and expanded by others in the community

---

## 👁️ Core Concept

The False Hydra is a **psychic predator** that feeds on towns and cities **without anyone realizing it’s there**.

### Its defining traits:

* **It sings.** A psychic song that suppresses **all memory and awareness** of the creature itself.
* **People vanish**, and **no one notices**. Family, friends, even whole districts disappear — and no one remembers they ever existed.
* It **grows more heads** as it consumes more victims.
* It typically **burrows underground**, using long serpentine necks to feed from beneath.

This creates terrifying gameplay where players slowly realize that something is wrong:

* Clues don't add up
* Townsfolk have blank spots in their memory
* Buildings look lived-in but are empty
* The party might have forgotten one of their own NPCs was with them...

## 🎭 Using the False Hydra in a Campaign

### **Tone**: Psychological horror, paranoia, surrealism

### **Best Level Range**: Tier 2 (Levels 5–10)

### **Works Best In**:

* Small isolated towns or villages
* Areas where missing people can go unnoticed
* Mystery-heavy adventures or one-shots
* Parties that love investigation, character drama, or horror

---

## 🧩 Encounter Ideas

* A town with empty houses and an eerily cheerful populace.
* The party finds a journal referencing someone no one else remembers.
* One of the PCs starts to **forget an NPC companion** they’ve had for months.
* A player is replaced and **no one at the table notices** until it’s revealed.
* The song temporarily stops — and they **see the monster for the first time**.

---

## 🕯️ DM Warning

The False Hydra is **extremely effective** for horror, but it messes with **player trust** and **memory**, which can be disturbing or frustrating if not handled carefully.

Use safety tools like:

* **Session 0 discussions**
* **Lines & Veils**
* **The X-card**
* Open communication about horror themes

## 💀 TL;DR: What is the False Hydra?

> A homebrew horror monster that sings a psychic song to erase itself from memory, allowing it to **silently consume towns** and **erase lives** while no one notices. Players must break the song's power to see it — and stop it — before **they themselves are forgotten**.
