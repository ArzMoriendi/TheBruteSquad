## 🪐 **Node 3: Astral Menagerie**

### **Read-Aloud Description**

*The chamber opens into a vaulted gallery of crystalline cages suspended in midair. Each cage holds a beast of nightmare—astral predators, star-touched horrors, and void-spawned monstrosities—frozen in time like insects in amber. Their eyes, though unmoving, seem to follow you as you pass. The glassy stasis hums with a low vibration, and fine cracks begin to spiderweb across the surface… until, with a sharp report, one of the cages shatters.*

---

### **Encounter Setup**

When the PCs enter the room, **roll 1d3** to determine what emerges first:

1. **Astral Dreadnought Fragment** *(custom weakened version)*

   * HP: ~200 (instead of 297).
   * Resistances: retains force, psychic; drop antimagic cone.
   * Bite, Claw, and Psychic Projection attacks intact.
   * Theme: A **broken-off shard of an astral titan**, its body incomplete but still horrifying.

2. **Star Spawn Seer** (MToF, p.236)

   * Unstable prophet of the void, surrounded by fragments of its own foresight.
   * Its arrival warps light: reflections on the crystalline cages begin showing *future versions of the PCs falling in battle*.

3. **Void Scavenger Swarm** *(Star Spawn Mangler, reflavored)*

   * Imagine dozens of fractured, insectile voidlings pouring out of a cracked cage.
   * They leap and slash in maddening swarms, clawing at armor seams and eyes.

---

### **Hazard — Cage Instability**

* The crystalline cages lining the chamber are **fragile**.
* Whenever an area-of-effect spell or ability (fireball, shatter, thunderwave, etc.) deals damage within the chamber, roll **1d6**.

  * **1–2:** One random cage cracks further.
  * **3–4:** A cage splinters (harmless visual effect).
  * **5–6:** Another monster begins to stir—add a second creature to the initiative after 1d4 rounds.

*This forces the party to fight tactically, avoiding overkill spells that could overwhelm them.*

---

### **Tactics & Feel**

* The released creature(s) fight desperately—their stasis has left them half-starved and maddened.
* The Lattice itself seems to treat the combat as a test: *can the intruders survive chaos when order collapses?*
* If the PCs linger too long, the **room itself destabilizes**, cracking more cages at DM’s discretion.

---

### **Victory Conditions**

* **Defeat or contain the creature(s).**

  * Containment ideas: *Wall of Force*, *Banishment*, or sealing a cage with **Arcana check DC 18** and spell slot expenditure.
* PCs who destroy **3 cages or more** risk overwhelming encounters unless they retreat.

---

### **Reward**

As the last foe falls, a **gentle light flares** from one of the shattered cages. Instead of a monster, this one holds only a chain of woven starlight—links faintly glowing like fireflies.

* **Item:** *Fragment of Sylvie’s Starlit Chain*

  * A piece of the bonds that hold her within the Lattice.
  * When touched, it pulses with warmth, and Sylvie’s voice briefly cuts through:
    *“You’re close… I can feel you.”*
  * Mechanically: Grants the Codex alignment to **Node 4**, allowing further passage.

---

### **Narrative Prompts for Roleplay**

* As cages crack, the reflections inside may whisper the PCs’ names or imitate loved ones.
* When the creature is defeated, describe the sound of cracking glass *ceasing*—as if the chamber itself exhales.
* Sylvie’s voice should grow progressively stronger each node, making her rescue feel imminent.
