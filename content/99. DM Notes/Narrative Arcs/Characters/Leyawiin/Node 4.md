## 🌀 Node 4: Spiral of Unraveling

### **Read-Aloud Description**

*The chamber opens into a spiraling shaft that stretches upward into infinity. Latticed chains, glowing with runes of paradox, coil upward in a twisting helix. The space between is weightless void. Yet gravity itself feels broken here—one moment tugging you upward, the next sideways, the next down into endless dark. The air trembles with warped echoes: boots marching, swords clashing, voices shouting in a language that seems both ancient and immediate.*

---

### **Challenge Layers**

#### 1. **Traversal Hazard — Gravity Flux**

* At the start of each PC’s turn:

  * They must make an **Athletics or Acrobatics check, DC 16** to control their movement and avoid violent slams into the chains.
  * **Fail:** The PC takes **4d10 force + psychic damage** as their body twists unnaturally against the flux.
  * **Succeed by 5+:** PC can use the momentum to leap/climb an extra 20 ft without spending movement.

* **Optional Party Strategy:**

  * *Countermeasures:* *Feather Fall*, *Fly*, or clever rope harnesses can mitigate danger.
  * PCs may attempt **Arcana DC 17** or **Insight DC 15** to anticipate the flux pattern, giving the group **advantage** on the next round of saves.

---

#### 2. **Combat Encounter — Time-Echo Raiders**

* Midway up the spiral, the party hears armored boots and guttural shouts.

* From the warped void, **faded Githyanki warriors** emerge—*not ghosts, but temporal echoes* of raiders who once tried and failed to breach the Lattice.

* **Stats:** Use **Githyanki Warrior (MM p.160)**.

  * Adjust narrative: Their armor is rusted, their blades cracked, their faces pale with centuries of unraveling time.
  * They fight with desperation, sometimes flickering into childlike or elderly versions of themselves mid-swing.

* **Combat Features:**

  * Raiders are affected by the **Gravity Flux** too. On initiative count 20, roll for a **gravity shift**:

    * 1–2: Everyone pulled **upward** 30 ft (Dex save DC 15 to grab a chain or float adrift).
    * 3–4: Gravity pulls **sideways**, slamming creatures into walls (2d10 bludgeoning).
    * 5–6: Gravity **neutralizes** (weightless for 1 round).

* Their defeat causes them to *fade into motes of dust and starlight*, their whispers: *“Free us… finish what we could not…”*

---

#### 3. **Victory Condition & Reward**

* PCs must survive traversal, overcome the raiders, and climb to the chamber’s apex.
* At the top, a massive rune-bound keystone floats in the void: the **Lattice Sigil**.

  * *Appearance:* A spinning octagonal glyph, each side inscribed with paradoxical runes that seem to rewrite themselves when looked at directly.
  * *Function:* This sigil is the key to unlocking the **final chamber of Sylvie’s prison**.
  * *When touched,* it brands each PC faintly with a glowing mark, proof of passage.

---

### **Narrative Prompts for Roleplay**

* As the raiders appear, you can flavor dialogue as overlapping temporal fragments:

  * *“This time, we win—no, no, it’s already lost…”*
  * *“You carry the chain… don’t drop it…”*
  * *“We died here once. We die here again.”*
* During the flux shifts, describe gravity viscerally:

  * *Your stomach lurches as you are yanked sideways, the shaft becoming a spinning hallway of impossible angles.*
* When the **Lattice Sigil** is claimed, Sylvie’s voice flickers through:

  * *“I feel it… the chains weakening. Hurry, before they notice…”*

---

✅ **Success:** PCs secure the **Lattice Sigil**, ensuring they can breach the final prison node.
❌ **Failure:** If they fall too far or trigger too many flux failures, they might plummet into temporal void—potentially landing in an earlier node (a soft fail with narrative consequence).
