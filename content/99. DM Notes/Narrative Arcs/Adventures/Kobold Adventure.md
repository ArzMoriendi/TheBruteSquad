### **The Emberforge Outpost – Qarux’s Brilliant Tinkerings**

Nestled in a rocky cleft of the **Crags**, behind a concealed stonefall and under tangled pines, lies a haphazard, smoke-wreathed kobold outpost known as the **Emberforge**. This curious den, filled with strange buzzing contraptions and the scent of singed fur, is the domain of **Qarux**—a cheerful, wide-eyed inventor with a thousand ideas and zero caution.

Once a beloved oddball in the kobold tribe of the **Neverwinter Woods**, Qarux’s constant tinkering eventually pushed the patience of the tribe’s elders. His inventions—harmless but loud—attracted too many owlbears, too often. After a steam-powered nutcracker flattened a sacred shrine, he was gently encouraged to **"set up a nice, quiet lab somewhere far, far away."**

Qarux took exile with a grin and a tail wag, forming the **Emberforge Outpost**, a sanctioned (but isolated) offshoot of the main tribe. He wasn’t sent alone—**the tribe assigned a few handlers** to keep an eye on him. These included:

* **Ding**, the gruff scout leader and de facto manager of the outpost. While officially second to Qarux, Ding keeps everything running smoothly and steps in when Qarux’s experiments get too explosive.
* **Scil**, a twitchy sorcerer whose wild magic often adds... surprises to Qarux’s devices. Scil claims the inventions "talk to him," which nobody has questioned for fear of becoming a test subject.
* **Kud, Vend, and Shrim**, loyal (if scatterbrained) scouts who serve as runners, lookouts, and glorified button-pushers.
* **Egga**, the flinty Den Mother who oversees food, rest, and discipline. She treats Qarux like a wayward child—one she secretly adores but would never admit it.
* **Areh**, the sharp-eyed and sharp-tongued scout who actually believes in Qarux’s vision. She's one of the few who speaks to him as an equal.

### **The Emberheart (or whatever it’s becoming)**

Qarux has no specific goal—he’s not trying to weaponize magic or conquer the surface. He just wants to **see what happens when you add gears to enchantment and pulleys to power**. He calls his current project “the **Emberheart**,” though it changes names weekly. To him, it’s part generator, part hearth, part musical instrument, and part “friend.”

He collects parts for it from the world above, scavenging or salvaging whatever his scouts can find. And two nights ago, **fortune smiled on the Emberforge**.

### **A Very Successful (and Shiny) Heist**

While on routine scouting, Ding and his team spotted a group of surface-dwellers camped near the Crags. Quiet, well-supplied, and visibly distracted by their campfire stew.

During the night, the kobolds crept in. Their **criteria for theft? Anything shiny, easy to carry, or with moving parts**. Scil distracted the camp with flickering lights and muffled sounds, while Shrim rolled through the bedrolls.

Among the haul:

* **Leyawiin’s spellbook, dagger, parchment, and lamp** – stolen simply because the cover gleamed and the pages smelled good.
* **Bronn’s lamp, dice, and wineskin** – the dice were “shiny with meaning,” according to Scil. The wineskin was "probably a battery."
* **Zwargie and Shadow Scale’s hunting traps and waterskins** – metallic and clunky. Perfect raw materials.
* **The party’s entire coin pouch (381 gold)** – glittering treasure, sure, but now mostly **wired into an unstable gear array**.
* **Veldar’s banana hammock** – pilfered not for utility, but because **Kud declared it “morale cloth of leadership.”** Qarux wears it on his head as a thinking band.

Now, Qarux is joyfully cobbling these artifacts into the Emberheart. He has no idea what half of them do, but that’s never stopped him. The spellbook is unreadable to him, but he's glued a page to a spinning copper plate "to see what happens when words go fast."

### **The Neverwinter Woods Connection**

The **main kobold tribe lives deep in the Neverwinter Woods**, nestled in root-dens beneath the mossy floor. Emberforge is technically a part of their network, but Qarux’s outpost is considered an “experimental anomaly.” Every few months, Ding sends a report back via coded bark parchment, and the elders roll their eyes and hope nothing explodes.

However, the **tribe is watching**. If Qarux’s work starts to show real promise—something that could warm dens, power tools, or even protect hatchlings—the elders may **move to reclaim his research**, and possibly **reintegrate (or control) him.** This gives you a solid link to a wider political plot down the line.

### **The Trail of Clues**

The adventuring party, woken to a chilly morning and the scent of missing dignity, followed **a winding trail of tiny claw prints**, **drag marks**, and **sticky wine patches** leading into the Crags. They now stand at the threshold of the Emberforge, hearing strange hums and high-pitched laughter echoing from within.

### **Potential Hooks and Outcomes**

* Qarux is open to **talking**. He’ll give items back... if he remembers what went where.
* **Areh** may urge the party to see the good in Qarux’s work—and hint at greater tensions with the tribe.
* **Scil** might accidentally activate a magical loop within the Emberheart, triggering an unstable energy pulse.
* **Ding** wants no trouble—just to get everyone out alive and get Qarux back to work.
* If the party breaks the Emberheart, they may earn Qarux’s confusion, not his anger. **He’ll just start again**, with their boots this time.

## 🗺️ **Emberforge Outpost – Cave Layout**

### **Overall Theme:**

A rocky, twisting kobold warren built into a cliffside in the Crags. The outer caves are rough and functional, while the deeper chambers reflect **Qarux's chaotic brilliance**—filled with strange contraptions, sparking wires, whirring gears, and scrap piles. Many areas are illuminated by stolen lamps or glowing mushrooms.

---

### **1. Entrance Tunnel (Guard Post)**

* **Narrow passage** with loose rocks and natural cover.
* **Trap:** A non-lethal swinging bucket full of rocks (DC 12 Dex save or 1d4 bludgeoning + prone).
* **Occupants:** **Kud and Shrim**, hiding behind a rock ledge with slings.
* **Features:** A crude alarm system (bell on a string) leading deeper into the warren.

---

### **2. Scout Barracks (Sleeping Alcoves)**

* A side chamber with **three sleeping nests**, old furs, and fish bones.
* **Hidden Cache (DC 13 Perception):** 1d4 minor stolen items (perhaps some parchment or Bronn’s dice).
* Wall carvings depict surface-dwellers wearing oversized kobold heads ("infiltration plans").

---

### **3. Central Commons / Cooking Pit**

* A wide chamber with a low fire pit, cracked cauldrons, and hanging fungus.
* A **repurposed waterskin** serves as a soup bladder (from Shadow Scale’s gear).
* **Areh and Egga** may be found here keeping the younger kobolds in line.
* **Morale Decor:** Veldar’s banana hammock now **hangs like a battle banner** above the fire.

---

### **4. Scil’s Arcane Nook**

* Tucked off the commons, this jagged alcove is lined with:

  * **Crude chalk glyphs**
  * A broken wand tip
  * Pieces of **Leyawiin’s spellbook**, with pages pinned to the walls
* **Wild Magic Hazard (optional):** If the Emberheart destabilizes, magic here reacts chaotically.

---

### **5. Workshop – The Emberheart Chamber (Qarux’s Lab)**

* The largest and deepest room, carved into a volcanic stone vent.
* **Steam vents** hiss from cracks in the floor (minor burn hazard).
* Dominating the center is the **Emberheart**, a glowing cobbled-together device of brass, bone, melted gold, and mystery:

  * Spinning gears made from **hunting trap parts**
  * A polished **dagger** set as a toggle switch
  * Spellbook pages moving on a gear like a prayer wheel
* **Qarux** is here constantly tinkering, assisted by **Vend**, while **Scil** hovers nearby.
* **Optional encounter hazard:** The Emberheart begins to **overload or vibrate** when disturbed.

---

### **6. Hidden Tunnel to the Surface / Emergency Exit**

* Behind a false wall (DC 14 Investigation), there’s a crawlspace leading out to a high ledge.
* Used by scouts to escape if attacked.
* Could serve as an **infiltration point** if the party scouts ahead or circles around.
