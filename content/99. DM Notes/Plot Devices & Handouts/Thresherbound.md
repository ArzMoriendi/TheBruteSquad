## 🎯 Core Idea: What the Pale Flame Wants

> **He Who Waits at the Threshold** wants to *undermine the finality of death* and *blur the boundary between soul and shell*. He is not trying to conquer the gods — he is trying to **rewrite the metaphysical rules** by which they operate.

Thus, he asks for **things that test or erode the distinction between the living and the dead**, especially when it comes to **souls, memory, and sacred transitions**.

These tasks would appear **morally ambiguous** at first… but slowly unravel the divine order from within.

## 🩸 What the Pale Flame Wants — Subtle but Theologically Subversive Requests

Each of these tasks sets your player at odds with Lathander and Kelemvor *ideologically* — but not immediately visibly.

---

### 1. **Souls Denied Passage**

**Task**: Collect the **echoes** of those who died *clinging to life* — those who refused to pass on, died in denial, or whose deaths were considered unjust. These souls do not ascend properly, and the Pale Flame offers them a new existence… through your character.

**Why it matters**: Kelemvor’s domain is violated — souls are meant to *pass*, not linger. By salvaging them, the PC denies the final rest.

---

### 2. **Unfinished Rites**

**Task**: Interrupt or steal away funerary rites — cremation ash, burial tokens, or consecrated ground. The PC doesn’t defile them, but *removes* the spiritual safeguard.

**Why it matters**: Without proper rites, Lathander’s light can’t reach the soul, and Kelemvor cannot guide it. The Pale Flame harvests what remains: rootless echoes. The act is subtle sabotage.

---

### 3. **Last Breath Vessels**

**Task**: Capture the *last breath* of mortally wounded individuals in special soul-binding vessels before they die. Even if they would normally receive divine care, the soul cannot fully escape once bottled.

**Why it matters**: This cheats both gods — the life (Lathander) is withheld, and the death (Kelemvor) is delayed or redirected.

---

### 4. **Guardians of the Threshold**

**Task**: Deliver the names or remnants of those who have narrowly escaped death — people who should have died but didn’t (e.g., someone miraculously healed or revived). Their names are etched on a tablet within the Pale Flame’s domain.

**Why it matters**: The Pale Flame marks these as his, laying claim to souls that Lathander might view as "saved" and Kelemvor as "due." It's metaphysical bookkeeping… with blasphemous implications.

---

### 5. **Memories of the Dead**

**Task**: Absorb or collect memories *about* the dead from the living — through dreams, stolen journals, whispered secrets, or even magically drawn recollections.

**Why it matters**: In the Forgotten Realms cosmology, memory helps *preserve* a soul’s essence in the afterlife. Stripping them away weakens the soul’s identity, disrupting Kelemvor’s judgment and Lathander’s blessings on legacy.

---

### 6. **The Hollowed**

**Task**: Guide others toward becoming Hollow Ones like the PC — those who die but are *re-lit* by the Pale Flame rather than returned to natural death.

**Why it matters**: Lathander sees resurrection as sacred. Kelemvor sees undeath as corruption. Hollow Ones defy both, yet are not *evil*. The more that exist, the more the threshold frays.

---

### 7. **Desecrated Dawn**

**Task**: Once in a while, the Pale Flame may ask you to **be present** at a sunrise — not to prevent it, but to *witness it* on his behalf. You feel a strange energy ripple as light passes through you unnaturally.

**Why it matters**: Lathander’s dawn is not meant to touch the Hollow. The act of standing in it — and being changed by it — is a theological paradox that *erodes* the symbolic purity of dawn.

## 🌘 Thematic Payoff

These tasks build tension slowly — temples may begin noticing irregularities:

* Spirits go missing.
* Undead begin manifesting personalities they shouldn’t have.
* Funerary rituals no longer “feel right.”
* Clerics of Kelemvor or Lathander may sense a **wrongness** around the character… but have no clear evidence.

Eventually, the PC may have to face:

* **Lathander’s clergy** questioning their “unlife.”
* **Kelemvorite inquisitors** asking where the souls went.
* **The Pale Flame offering more power** — but only in exchange for bigger sacrifices.

### Suggestions for The Pale Flame’s Requests to the Elf Warlock

Here’s a list of **gradual, subtle tasks** The Pale Flame might assign that create shades of tension, questioning faith, and testing party bonds:

---

## 1. **Retrieve "Lost Echoes"**

* **Task:** Collect objects or mementos linked to souls who died suddenly or violently but were not given proper rites (e.g., a bloodied personal item from a battlefield grave).
* **Why it causes tension:**

  * The Paladin sees honoring the dead and rites as sacred — retrieving such items might feel like disturbing sacred ground.
  * The Cleric worries about interrupting the natural passage of souls.
  * But the Warlock’s patron demands these items to “bind the lingering shadows.”
* **Subtle conflict:** Disagreement on whether the task is *helpful* or *harmful* to the dead’s memory and peace.

---

## 2. **Guard the Hollowed**

* **Task:** Protect or guide other Hollow Ones or revenant spirits the Pale Flame revives or animates.
* **Why it causes tension:**

  * The Paladin’s oath forbids harming innocents but struggles with the idea of “unnatural” life.
  * The Cleric fears these beings upset the cosmic balance.
  * The Warlock becomes a caretaker of these liminal souls, blurring life and death.
* **Subtle conflict:** The party debates if the Hollow Ones are victims, abominations, or something in between.

---

## 3. **Deliver Last Whispers**

* **Task:** Collect whispered secrets or final confessions from dying individuals — by magical means or persuasion — and deliver them as “offerings” to the Pale Flame.
* **Why it causes tension:**

  * The Paladin values mercy and sacred rites, not probing final moments for power.
  * The Cleric views it as invading the sacred passage of souls to the afterlife.
  * The Warlock sees it as part of the pact and a way to honor those lost.
* **Subtle conflict:** Moral questions about privacy, consent, and exploitation of the dying.

---

## 4. **Twilight Vigils**

* **Task:** Attend or initiate vigils at crossroads or burial grounds during twilight hours — moments between day and night — to “hold the door open” for the Pale Flame’s influence.
* **Why it causes tension:**

  * The Paladin finds disturbing a natural order (sunrise/sunset) unholy.
  * The Cleric warns about disrupting the cycle of life and death.
  * The Warlock feels empowered, but unsure if the ritual is right or wrong.
* **Subtle conflict:** Philosophical disagreement on interfering with the world’s rhythms.

---

## 5. **Retrieve Forgotten Names**

* **Task:** Find and record the names of those who have died anonymously or were erased from memory (orphans, lost soldiers, forgotten victims).
* **Why it causes tension:**

  * The Paladin believes all lives deserve remembrance but worries about meddling in fates.
  * The Cleric believes forgetting is part of death’s mercy.
  * The Warlock’s patron wants these names to call them back or bind their souls.
* **Subtle conflict:** Questions of remembrance, dignity, and who has the right to intervene in souls’ fates.

---

## 6. **Harvest Fragments of Light**

* **Task:** Capture tiny traces of light from sunrise or dawn — metaphorically the blessing of Lathander — but only fleeting fragments to empower the Pale Flame’s threshold.
* **Why it causes tension:**

  * The Paladin’s god would see this as sacrilege, but it’s subtle enough to be overlooked.
  * The Cleric feels the balance is fragile and fears consequences.
  * The Warlock feels caught between the divine powers of dawn and death.
* **Subtle conflict:** The Warlock is literally taking from Lathander’s domain for a shadow god — a silent betrayal.
