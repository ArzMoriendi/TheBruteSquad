# 💀 RP SCENE: “A Week Too Long”

## 📍 Location

A cramped, stone-walled backroom in Mirabar’s trade quarter. Functional, not comfortable. The kind of place where goods are exchanged, not discussed.

The party arrives with a **sealed leather bag containing a human head (Kragnaroth the Drunk)** that has been stored for **one week with minimal preservation**.

Everyone in the room understands immediately:  
This is not a pristine bounty proof. This is **rot-adjusted currency**.

Daeric Elmlight has arranged the meeting through loose contacts—this is a **pass-through deal**, not a trusted chain.

---

# 🪨 NPC: MARMOREAL (Earth Genasi Broker)

Marmoreal

Marmoreal is present only to **facilitate introduction, not guarantee value**. His involvement is minimal and deliberately noncommittal.

### Reaction

He glances at the bag, then at the party.

> “A week in leather is not preservation. It is negotiation with time.”

He does not assess value. He only confirms:

- Theriggs will see it
    
- No one here is lying about what it is _supposed_ to be
    

Then he steps back—emotionally and physically.

---

# 🪓 NPC: THERIGGS DARKJAW (Duergar Fence)

Theriggs Darkjaw

Theriggs is the **actual decision point**.

He is not impressed, not offended—just pragmatic.

### Reaction to the Bag

He does not open it immediately.

> “You waited a week. That costs you.”

When he inspects it:

- Identity is acceptable
    
- Condition is degraded
    
- Value is immediately adjusted downward
    

He is not arguing legitimacy—he is pricing **decay and inconvenience**

---

# ⚖️ CORE DEAL STRUCTURE (VERY IMPORTANT)

## 🎯 Party Target:

**250 gp total payout**

This is a **quick flip / pass-through bounty exchange**, not a full valuation negotiation.

## 💰 Theriggs Base Offer:

He will _start low_ due to condition risk:

- **Base opening offer:** 150 gp
    
- Because:
    
    - decomposition risk
        
    - verification uncertainty
        
    - resale/claim processing hassle
        
    - “week-old proof” penalty

# 🎲 NEGOTIATION CHECKS (FOCUSED ON VALUE RECOVERY)

## 1. **Persuasion (Primary Value Recovery)**

Convince Theriggs the delay is normal field handling

- DC 12 → Raises offer to ~200 gp
    
- DC 15 → Reaches **250 gp target**
    
- DC 18+ → 275–300 gp (premium “no-argument” acceptance)
    

Failure:

- Stays at 150–175 gp
    

---

## 2. **Insight (Read his pricing pressure)**

- DC 10 → He’s not emotional, just pricing risk
    
- DC 13 → He wants to close fast; low patience for haggling
    
- DC 16 → He actually has multiple similar buys this week and is standardizing payouts low
    

---

## 3. **Deception (Optional framing of condition)**

Used if players justify decay:

- Failure → He increases discounting pressure (drops offer 25–50 gp)
    

---

## 4. **Performance (Soft negotiation tone)**

- DC 12 → keeps negotiation smooth, no friction loss
    
- DC 15 → prevents Theriggs from “anchoring low”
    
- DC 18 → nudges him into treating 250 gp as “efficient closure point”
