**Boniface Abernathy, Patron of Adventurers**

---

**Race**: Human  
**Class**: (Influence, Entrepreneur)  
**Alignment**: Lawful Evil (Outwardly Charismatic, Inwardly Ruthless)  
**Age**: 47  
**Height**: 6'4"  
**Build**: Broad-chested, muscular frame with a noticeable paunch  
**Hair**: Fiery red, thinning over a bald spot on the crown of his head  
**Eyes**: Light green, twinkling with calculated ambition

---

### **Physical Description**

Boniface Abernathy is a striking figure at first glance—a tall, broad-chested man with a commanding presence. His thick red hair, though thinning in patches, remains proudly atop his head, often slicked back with an extra layer of pomade that adds an exaggerated sheen to his appearance. His face is ruddy, as though he’s spent years basking in the sun, but it's the bright green eyes that captivate most who meet him—they glint with an energy that borders on mischievous, almost as if he knows something you don’t. His laughter is loud and contagious, often filling a room, and his mannerisms are bold and expressive, a true performer.

He dresses well, but with a deliberate flamboyance that screams "I have money to spare" while not being too ostentatious. His tailored coats, embroidered with subtle gold thread, and shiny boots are always perfectly maintained. However, there's a touch of discomfort about him—his wardrobe is slightly too tight around the chest and waist, as though he’s still trying to project an image of the adventurer he once was, even though the body has long since outgrown it.

Despite his outward joviality, there’s a tension in his smile—a predatory glint behind the laughter, like a merchant counting the coins in his pocket when shaking hands. He may be the life of the party, but his mind is always calculating the next step.

---

### **Backstory**

Boniface Abernathy was born the eldest son of a once-proud merchant family that saw its fortunes slip through its fingers during the **Spellplague**. As a child, he was known for his clever tongue and an almost unnatural charisma, traits that endeared him to others, though his parents saw them as a potential tool for manipulating the family’s situation. His father, a hard-nosed merchant with a sharp business mind, groomed him to take over the family business, but Boniface had loftier ambitions.

Rather than simply managing a dying trade empire, he was inspired by stories of adventurers—heroes who found treasure, power, and glory in places beyond the mundane world. As a young man, he set out to forge his own path, making a name for himself as a guide and patron for adventurers. He saw the endless potential in the business of treasure hunting: the thrill of gold, the excitement of a newly discovered ruin, and the absolute control over those willing to risk their lives for wealth.

Abernathy's rise began when he sponsored a group of adventurers in the **Ruins of Xalanthar**, a long-forgotten dwarven stronghold deep in the **Anauroch Desert**. The adventurers returned victorious, laden with riches, and Boniface saw how easy it was to profit from the brave, naïve fools who craved glory. By providing them with resources, maps, and safe havens, he quickly built a small network of adventurers, mercenaries, and treasure hunters, all working under his watchful eye.

Though his adventurers were often successful, they were little more than tools in Boniface’s larger plan. The man behind the curtain, he began taking a hefty percentage of their earnings, sometimes up to 50%—or more, if a job went sideways. He learned how to manipulate contracts, twist promises, and convince adventurers to take greater risks for a share of the potential rewards. Over time, his network expanded from small-time treasure hunters to larger mercenary bands, political operatives, and even **rogue wizards** with questionable allegiances.

Boniface established his operations in **Waterdeep**, leveraging its influence and wealth to secure powerful patrons and allies, using his charm to smooth over rough edges when necessary. Over the years, his influence spread, and he soon became one of the most recognized—and feared—patrons in the **Sword Coast**. He is now the owner of a lavish mansion near the **City of Splendors** and regularly hosts grand soirées attended by nobles, adventurers, and influential figures.

### **Personality**

Outwardly, Boniface is the embodiment of warmth and charisma. He is **gregarious**, **loquacious**, and easily approachable, effortlessly gliding through conversations while making people feel like they are the center of attention. His handshake is firm, his compliments genuine—at least, they seem genuine. He’s the type of person who laughs loudly at others’ jokes, often repeating the punchline for effect. His voice has the boisterous charm of a **traveling merchant** who’s had one too many drinks in celebration of his latest success.

However, this persona is a carefully constructed mask. Beneath the jovial exterior lies a **ruthless**, **business-minded** predator. Boniface’s **endgame** is power—wealth is just the means to get there. He is adept at using people for his own gain, **enslaving adventurers** with contracts, exploiting their fear of failure to keep them in line. He’s quick to turn on anyone who doesn’t serve his interests, whether they are close friends or hired swords. **Deception** and **manipulation** are his tools, and he’s not afraid to throw someone under the bus if it means a little extra profit.

Boniface is a man with **ambitious goals**. He is constantly looking for ways to expand his empire—whether through adventurer guilds, buying up land in remote regions, or even clandestine dealings with **shadowy organizations**. His endgame is to establish himself as the preeminent **power broker** of Faerûn, **controlling both the legitimate and illicit economies**. He is particularly interested in obtaining relics of immense power, thinking of them less as magical artifacts and more as investments that will elevate his standing.

---

### **Notable Quirks**

- **Endless Compliments**: Boniface never misses an opportunity to flatter those he meets, whether it’s a potential client, an adventurer, or even a rival. However, he is a master of **backhanded compliments**—always managing to leave his target feeling a little more self-conscious than when they arrived.
    
- **Obsession with Expanding Influence**: Boniface often casually mentions new schemes and projects he’s working on—most of which are either overly ambitious or completely unethical. He has a tendency to belabor the importance of **'growth'** and **'networking'**, often offering advice to adventurers that subtly nudges them into more dangerous (and profitable) opportunities.
    
- **Loves to Laugh**: Boniface laughs—loudly and often. Whether it’s at his own jokes or someone else’s, it’s his way of disarming others. His laughter is almost infectious, but there’s something unsettling about how **calculated** it is.
    

---

### **Boniface’s Role in the Campaign**

Boniface serves as a **patron** for adventurers, always ready with quests that promise vast riches and glory—so long as they adhere to his contracts. He may offer the party jobs, but those jobs will always come with strings attached, some more subtle than others.

If the adventurers find themselves in need of resources, Boniface is always available, but they will quickly discover that his “generosity” comes at a steep price. He might have an interest in using the adventurers to acquire powerful magic items, uncover lost kingdoms, or further his business ventures. The party might also discover his involvement in darker dealings—connections to criminal networks, shady mercenaries, or even secret alliances with nefarious cults.

Ultimately, Boniface could either serve as a long-term ally, **manipulating the party for his own gain**, or a **dangerous rival**, especially if the adventurers interfere with his operations. His goals are always aimed at **self-enrichment**, and **betrayal** is never far from his mind.

### **Boniface’s Residence and Offices

Boniface runs the **Luminous Meridian Trade Company**, which operates primarily out of **Neverwinter**, but has satellite offices in other major cities including **Waterdeep** and **Baldur's Gate**. Much like the Dutch East India Trading Company, they operate ruthlessly and are set on expansion, exploration, and power.

### **Traits**

**Leader’s Aura** (Recharge 5-6).  
Boniface can use his **Leader's Aura** to bolster his allies. As a bonus action, he can choose one creature within 30 feet of him (other than himself) that can see or hear him. That creature gains temporary hit points equal to Boniface's Charisma modifier (minimum of 1) + his Fighter level (16). These temporary hit points last for 1 minute.

**Business Savvy**.  
Boniface has advantage on Charisma (Deception) and Charisma (Persuasion) checks when dealing with business transactions, trades, or attempts to gain influence over others.

**Brutal Tactician**.  
Boniface can use his action to carefully assess a battlefield. He can grant one ally within 30 feet of him advantage on their next attack roll, as long as that ally can see or hear him. Once he does this, he cannot use this feature again until after a short or long rest.

**Fighting Style (Dueling)**.  
Boniface has mastered dual-wielding Shamshirs. When wielding two light weapons, Boniface gains a +2 bonus to damage rolls with his off-hand weapon.

---

### **Actions**

**Multiattack**.  
Boniface makes three melee attacks with his **Shamshirs**.

**Dual Shamshir Strike** (Melee Weapon Attack): +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.  
Hit: 11 (1d6 + 4) slashing damage for the main-hand attack.  
Hit: 10 (1d6 + 4) slashing damage for the off-hand attack (with dueling style bonus).

**Silver-tongued Strike** (Recharge 5-6).  
Boniface can use his words as a weapon, briefly incapacitating or charming a target. One creature within 30 feet of him that can hear him must succeed on a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw or be **charmed** for 1 minute. While charmed, the creature cannot take hostile actions against Boniface and will be inclined to help him, though it can attempt to break the charm once per turn by making a new Wisdom saving throw at the end of each of its turns. This effect can be broken if the target is attacked or harmed by someone Boniface is allied with. After using this ability, Boniface cannot use it again until after a short or long rest.

**Fighter’s Precision** (Recharge 6).  
As a bonus action, Boniface can sharpen his instincts for a single attack. He can choose one attack roll he makes within the next minute to automatically hit, but the damage dealt is halved.

**Action Surge** (1/Short Rest).  
Boniface can take one additional action on his turn.

---

### **Reactions**

**Parry**.  
Boniface can use his reaction to reduce the damage of a melee attack that hits him. He reduces the damage by 5 (1d6 + 4).

**Defensive Duelist** (1/Day).  
When a creature attacks Boniface with a melee weapon attack, he can use his reaction to add +4 to his AC against that attack, potentially causing it to miss.

---

### **Legendary Actions** (3/Day)

Boniface can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. Boniface regains spent legendary actions at the start of his turn.

1. **Quick Strike**. Boniface makes one melee attack with his Shamshir.
    
2. **Mocking Words**. Boniface targets one creature within 30 feet of him that can hear him. The creature must succeed on a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw or be **disadvantaged** on their next attack roll.
    
3. **Commanding Presence** (Costs 2 Actions). Boniface can command an ally within 30 feet of him to take a reaction on their turn, such as attacking or moving.
    

---

### **Legendary Resistance (1/Day)**

If Boniface fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.

### **Personality**

Boniface Abernathy is **charming** and **manipulative**, with a keen mind for business and the art of influence. His laughter is booming and **infectious**, but his true nature is one of ruthless calculation. Though he uses his charisma to build relationships and trust, Boniface is always thinking one step ahead, using his connections, manipulation, and wealth to further his personal goals of **power** and **influence**. Beneath his jovial exterior, he is a **cold and calculating strategist**, and every adventurer, noble, or ally is simply a tool for his grander ambitions.

---

### **Combat Style**

Boniface’s combat prowess is as refined as his business acumen. As a seasoned fighter, he uses his **dual Shamshirs** to deal swift, precise strikes, cutting down opponents with a mix of speed, dexterity, and lethal force. With his **Dueling Fighting Style**, he is incredibly effective when wielding his weapons, capitalizing on each strike to deal damage with both hands while protecting himself with strategic use of **Parry** and **Defensive Duelist**. His legendary actions, like **Mocking Words** and **Commanding Presence**, allow him to control the battlefield by influencing his enemies and allies alike.

His **Silver-tongued Strike** ability allows him to twist the battlefield with both words and blades, using his charm to cause enemies to hesitate or even aid him in battle.

As a **Level 16 Fighter**, Boniface excels not only in **melee combat** but also in **tactical leadership**. His **Brutal Tactician** and **Leader’s Aura** abilities make him a vital part of any skirmish, improving the odds of his allies while ensuring his **long-term plans** always have the upper hand.
