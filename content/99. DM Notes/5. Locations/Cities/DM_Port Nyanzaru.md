## 🏙️ General Overview & Population

Port Nyanzaru is the **only functioning city in [[Chult]]**, situated on the southern coast of the Bay of Chult and surrounded by dense jungle. The other cities in the region are ruins or overrun (Mezro, Omu, etc.) ([World Anvil][1]).

The population is mostly **ethnic Chultans (human)**—about two‑thirds—alongside **humans of Amnian descent** from the colonial period. Other races found include **dwarves (especially albino dwarf clans), elves, halflings, dragonborn**, and **“beastfolk”** such as **Tabaxi, tortles, aarakocra**, and even some goblinoid visitors. Many of these have assimilated into trade and city life, often escaping jungle dangers ([World Anvil][2]).

---

## 🏛️ Government

Power lies with the **seven Merchant Princes**, each a wealthy Chultan trader who controls a particular trade or service sector. They rule collectively as a council seated at **Goldenthrone**, an opulent palace overlooking the harbor ([World Anvil][2]).

---

## 🏞️ City Layout & Districts

### **Harbor Ward**

* Located on the northern waterfront, it contains the **Royal Docks**, **public docks**, a **dry dock** where dinosaur teams haul ships, warehouses, the Harbormaster’s offices, and **Fort Nyanzaru**—which can raise a harbor chain and house ballistae for defense. A lighthouse here also sends smoke signals across the Bay ([Forgotten Realms][3]).

### **Market Ward**

* Northeast of Harbor Ward. Home to the **Red Bazaar** (local produce, Chultan goods), the **Grand Souk** (imported goods and armor), **Jewel Markets**, **Hall of Gold**, public bathhouses, the **Grand Coliseum**, and the **Thundering Lizard** tavern ([Travelogue][4]).

### **Merchants’ Ward**

* Northwest, built on Temple Hill and Throne Hill. Contains **Goldenthrone Palace**, **Home of Beauty**, the **Temple of Inspiration Divine**, and upscale markets like the Grand Souk and jewel merchants ([Chronicles of Faerun][5]).

### **Old City**

* Lies outside the city walls (southwest), a poorer district plagued by **refuse pits** and undead incursions. Dominated by three ancient ziggurat temples and controlled informally by shadowy figures known as **Beggar Princes** (e.g. Pock‑Marked Po). It has frequent undead-related unsettling events during the Death Curse storyline ([Forgotten Realms][6]).

### **Tiryki Anchorage**

* Southeast outside the walls at the mouth of the Tiryki river. It’s a rough district of **animal‑handlers**, dinosaur stables, smugglers, and the dinosaur pens. Streets are narrow, dirty, and full of pungent odors of manure and tanned hides. It also features local scrub housing and taverns ([Forgotten Realms][7]).

### **Warehouse District**

* Just north of the Grand Souk, adjacent to Harbor Ward. A network of waterways enables dinosaurs to pull ships directly to warehouses and loading docks ([Forgotten Realms][3]).

---

## 📜 History & Context

* Originally founded in the **Mid‑1300s DR** by **Amnian colonists** at the mouth of the Soshenstar River, the city served as Chult’s colonial capital. Roughly **nine years before the events of Tomb of Annihilation**, the Chultans expelled Amnian control and established an independent city-state governed by Merchant Princes backed by the **Ytepka Society** and native elites ([World Anvil][1]).

* Omu and Mezro—once thriving cities—fell to undead and ancient machinations. Their memory has faded among ordinary citizens, partly because jungle travel is rare and the knowledge is guarded or taboo ([Reddit][8]).

---

## 🎭 Plot-Relevant Events in *Tomb of Annihilation*

* **Chapter 1** of the module introduces Port Nyanzaru as the starting hub. Players encounter **Merchant Princes** (such as Wakanga O’Tamu, Jobal, Ekene‑Afa, Jessamine, Kwayothé) and gain access to guides, magic items, dinosaur races, and side opportunities tied to the Death Curse saga ([Travelogue][4]).

* The city hosts **dinosaur races and gladiatorial games** (especially in the Coliseum), and is a thriving center of tourism and gamble-driven commerce. These entertainments are important hooks and symbols of Chultan culture under Merchant Prince rule ([World Anvil][1]).

* **Undead disturbances** during Chapter 1: citizens in Old City hear the “Call of the Dead” and vanish only to rise as zombies later. Later exploration reveals a necromantic plot by Jaru Tasca in the refuse pits beneath the Old City catacombs—the threat is rooted in local undead activity rather than just jungle horror ([Forgotten Realms][6]).

* **Security & threats**: while the city is fortified inside the walls, areas like Malar’s Throat and Old City are vulnerable to floods from ravines and to undead incursions. The city’s defense is maintained by mercenaries and the Merchant Princes rather than a formal army, and unsanctioned trade or guide work is dangerous and strictly regulated ([World Anvil][2]).

## 🧭 Overview & Geography

Port Nyanzaru serves as the **only major civilized settlement in Chult**, occupying the gentle coast of the **Bay of Chult** where the **River Soshenstar** meets the sea. It is encased within high stone walls, backed by dense and perilous jungle and patrolled waters. ([travelogue.fandom.com](https://travelogue.fandom.com/wiki/Tomb_of_Annihilation?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Tomb of Annihilation | The Travelogue Wiki | Fandom"))

## Government & Politics

Authority lies with the **seven Merchant Princes**, all Chultans and the city’s richest. They govern collectively from the **Goldenthrone palace**, enforce trade monopolies, and even authorize the rare legal purchases of sanctions. Their rule is balanced internally and moderated by societal checks like the **Ytepka Society**. ([tomb-of-annihilation.fandom.com](https://tomb-of-annihilation.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_Princes?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Merchant Princes | Tomb of Annihilation Wiki | Fandom"))

## Population & Size

Port Nyanzaru houses about **12,000** people in the late 1400s DR—significant in Chult as its largest urban center. Its cosmopolitan population includes multiple races and hidden cultists. Founded as an Amnian colony in the 14th century, Port Nyanzaru broke colonial bonds in the late 1480s, establishing merchant rule with the backing of the **Ytepka Society**. It became the pivotal base for adventurers arriving during the **Death Curse** crisis (1491 DR).
