## Backstory

Veldar is a massive Goliath from the Frostfang Tribe, born in the icy Spine of the World where strength was everything—and Veldar had plenty. His mind, however, was slow and foggy, like a frozen river. He was loyal, simple, and easily led, making him the perfect blunt-force soldier. Once, he guarded rocks from “sneaky clouds” for two days because someone told him to.

Veldar’s heart was bigger than his brain, and he loved his tribe fiercely. He’d share his last scrap of mammoth jerky, carry wounded warriors through blizzards, or hug a crying child until they giggled (or squirmed free). But everything changed during the Night of Shattered Peaks, when the Ironclaw Clan ambushed his tribe. Ordered to hold a mountain pass, Veldar fought bravely—only to realize too late that everyone else was gone. Veldar, too thick to grasp the scale of the loss, waited for days, calling out, “Chief? Where you go?” until hunger forced him to wander south.

In the lowlands, people took advantage of him. He was duped by merchants and misled by bandits, but Veldar never noticed—he just wanted to belong. His outbursts grew more violent when he felt hurt or tricked, smashing things while yelling, “YOU NO NICE!”

Now, Veldar roams in search of a new “family.” He’s a gentle brute—loyal, simple, and eager to please. He doesn’t get sarcasm or nuance, but he’ll protect anyone who shows him kindness. His fists hit as hard as ever, and though his grin remains, there's sadness behind it. He still dreams of hearing someone say, “Good job, Veldar.”
