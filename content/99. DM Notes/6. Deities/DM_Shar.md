## **Shar** — *Mistress of the Night, Lady of Loss*

### 📜 Titles & Alignment

* **Titles**: Mistress of the Night; Lady of Loss; Nightbringer; Dark Goddess; Lady of Forgetfulness; Singer of Eternal Night; Mistress of All That Is Hidden ([Forgotten Realms][1])
* **Alignment**: Neutral Evil ([Forgotten Realms][1], _Sword Coast Adventurer’s Guide_)

### 🛐 Portfolio & Domains

- **Portfolio**: Darkness, night, loss, forgetfulness, secrets, caverns, unrevealed places, pain, grief, nihilism, hidden things ([Forgotten Realms Wiki])
    
- **5e Domains**: _Death_, _Trickery_ (commonly associated in 5e-adapted play; older editions also included Evil and Knowledge) ([Sword Coast Adventurer’s Guide], Forgotten Realms canon traditions)
    

---

### 🎨 Symbol, Colors & Avatar

- **Symbol**: A black disk encircled with a purple border, often representing a dark moon eclipsing light ([Forgotten Realms Wiki])
    
- **Colors**: Black, purple, deep violet, silver-black hues associated with moonless night and mourning ([Forgotten Realms Wiki])
    
- **Divine Avatar**: Often appears as a tall, beautiful woman cloaked in flowing black shadow, with dark hair and eyes like empty voids. Her presence muffles sound and dims nearby light ([Forgotten Realms Wiki])
    

---

### 📖 Lore, History & Responsibilities

- Shar is one of the oldest deities of Toril, twin sister and eternal rival of **Selûne**. Together they emerged from primordial chaos, embodying darkness and light respectively ([Forgotten Realms Wiki]).
    
- She seeks to extinguish hope, erase memory, and return existence to endless darkness. Shar teaches that pain is inevitable, and release comes only through surrender and forgetfulness.
    
- Shar was central to the ancient conflict over the creation of the sun and stars, opposing Selûne’s gift of light to the world. Their feud remains one of the defining divine rivalries of the Realms.
    
- She created the **Shadow Weave**, a dark counterpart to Mystra’s Weave, granting secretive magic rooted in shadow and loss. Though destroyed after the Spellplague, its legacy remains infamous ([Forgotten Realms Wiki], 4e/5e Realms lore).
    
- Many worship Shar in secret cells, grief cults, hidden monasteries, or political conspiracies. Public worship is often feared or outlawed.

### 🤝 Allies & Adversaries

- **Allies**:
    
    - _Cyric_ (temporary historical alliance during divine upheaval)
        
    - _Mask_ (occasional shared interests in secrecy and shadow)
        
    - Dark sects, nihilistic cults, shadow organizations
        
- **Foes**:
    
    - _Selûne_ (eternal twin rival)
        
    - _Mystra_ (opposed through the Shadow Weave)
        
    - _Lathander_ (hope and renewal opposed to despair)
        
    - _Torm, Tyr, Ilmater_ and many benevolent gods who oppose cruelty and corruption
        

---

### 🧮 Practices & Worship

- **Worshipers**: The grieving, the vengeful, secret-keepers, spies, assassins, conspirators, those broken by loss, shadow sorcerers, disillusioned nobles, hidden cultists ([Forgotten Realms Wiki]).
    
- **Clerical Alignments**: Lawful Evil, Neutral Evil, Chaotic Evil (LE, NE, CE), sometimes true neutral servants focused on secrecy.
    
- **Favored Weapon**: _Darkbolt_ or ceremonial dagger/longsword traditions depending source and edition; knives and subtle weapons are common among her clergy ([Forgotten Realms Wiki]).
    
- **Holy Days & Rituals**:
    
    - _Feast of the Moon_ shadow rites held in darkness and silence
        
    - Midnight vigils for those consumed by grief
        
    - Ceremonies of forgetting, where names or memories are ritually surrendered
        
    - Secret initiations in unlit chambers illuminated only by purple candles
