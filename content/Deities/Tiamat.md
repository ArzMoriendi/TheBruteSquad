Here is an official D\&D 5e‑era profile of **Tiamat**, the draconic deity of evil and chromatic dragonkind, based solely on *Wizards of the Coast* 5e sources and canon-level lore via the *Forgotten Realms Wiki*:

---

## **Tiamat** — *The Dragon Queen, Queen of Chromatic Dragons*

### 🛐 **Portfolio, Domains & Responsibilities**

* **Portfolio**: Evil dragons and reptiles, conquest, greed, vengeance, law (as control), and tyranny. She is the ultimate patron of chromatic dragons across Faerûn and beyond ([Wikipedia][1], [realmshelps.net][2]).
* **5e Domains**: The *Player’s Handbook* specifies only **Trickery** for Tiamat; earlier editions included Law, Greed, Destruction, Tyranny, and Hatred—but only *Trickery* is canonical in 5e core material ([Reddit][3]). Casual lore may also attribute *War* or *Destruction*, but these are not formally codified in 5e.
* **Responsibilities**: She demands tribute and obedience from chromatic dragons and their mortal agents. Her cultists seek to destabilize civilization, propagate evil dragonkind, and accumulate power and treasure in her name.

---

### 🎨 **Symbols, Appearance & Avatar**

* **Symbol**: A stylized five‑headed dragon, each head the color of a chromatic dragon (white, black, green, blue, red) ([fallenwest.proboards.com][4]).
* **Appearance**: Tiamat manifests as a colossal five-headed dragon, each head spitting a different breath weapon. In her avatar or mortal disguise, rarely appears in humanoid form—typically a fearsome dragonspawn or disguise of a chromatic dragon ([planewalker.com][5]).
* **Home Plane**: Her realm is located in Baator's first layer (Avernus), often called the Dragonspawn Pits of Azharul. Her chamber lies within the Cave of Greed, guarded by cursed treasures and infernal constructs ([planewalker.com][5]).

---

### 📜 **History & Lore**

* **Divine Origins**: Daughter or aspect of Io (Asgorath), Tiamat represents the darker, destructive, and avaricious side of dragons. In some cosmologies she is martially opposed to Bahamut, her sibling and cosmic counterpart ([Wikipedia][1]).
* **Faerûnian Role**: Her resurgence in Faerûn came during the Tyranny of Dragons storyline (*Hoard of the Dragon Queen* and *Rise of Tiamat*) when cultists sought to summon her from Avernus. Though mortal champions partly defeated that scheme, her power and cult grew as a result ([Wikipedia][1]).
* **Significant Event**: One of her avatars was slain in 1359 DR by Gareth Dragonsbane to destroy the Wand of Orcus at Bahamut’s behest. Tiamat later rallied dragonspawn and agents to Vaasa and Luthcheq, eventually gaining minor deity status in Faerûn by 1371 DR ([Forgotten Realms][6]).

---

### ⚔️ **Adversaries & Allies**

* **Greatest Foe**: *Bahamut*, the Platinum Dragon and her cosmic antithesis; they stand on opposite poles of morality and leadership among dragonkind ([Wikipedia][1], [World Anvil][7]).
* **Allies & Agents**: Evil chromatic dragons, cultists (notably within the Cult of the Dragon), tyrants, treasure‑hunters, and demon-lords like Bel in Baator. She is known to collaborate with devils and evil deities for power expansion ([frc.fandom.com][8]).

---

### 🧭 **Worship, Clergy & Practices**

* **Worshipers**: Evil dragons (especially green and blue), chromatic dragon spawn, kobolds, evil dragonborn, vampire-savants, mercenaries, assassins, and those who idolize power and wealth. Clerics are usually LE or NE with LN allowed ([realmshelps.net][2]).
* **Clerical Hierarchy**: Complex ranks: **Custodians of the Copper Chalice**, **Defenders of the Silver Shield**, **Keepers of the Platinum Crown**, **Scales of the White Wyrm**, **Talons of the Blue Baatoran**, **Dark Scaly Ones**, etc. Cult leadership enforces rigid loyalty and ritual compliance ([Wikipedia][1]).
* **Temples & Shrines**: Hidden lairs or caverns filled with hoarded treasure; few grand temples in Faerûn. Shrines often mimic a dragon’s lair.
* **Rituals**:

  * *Tithing*: Buried donations offered at dusk, praying she will return the next dawn—a demonstration of faith and greed combined.
  * *Rite of Respect*: Required when approaching any chromatic dragon, failure or error in ceremony may incur wrath ([fallenwest.proboards.com][4], [frc.fandom.com][8]).
  * *Festival of Vengeance*: Celebrated after her defeat of Gilgeam in Unther; marked by mayhem, sabotage, and violence within chromatic-ruled regions ([Forgotten Realms][6]).
