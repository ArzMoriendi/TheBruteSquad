The Pale Flame, He Who Waits at the Threshold

_Hexblade Patron (Forgotten Realms – Warlock Subclass Option)_

> _"He stands where the soul hesitates. He offers fire to guide it back—pale, cold, and eternal."_

Among the cryptic pantheon of eldritch beings that brush the edges of mortal comprehension, one stands ever at the border of life and death. He is known by many names whispered in dread and reverence across ancient tombs, hidden cults, and the ramblings of the half-dead. Yet two names endure above the rest: **The Pale Flame**, and **He Who Waits at the Threshold**.

He is not a god, nor does he wish to be. He makes no claims upon the soul—only offers _passage_. He is a sentinel of the liminal, a force both radiant and empty, and those who call upon him learn that resurrection is not a mercy... but a transaction.

Unlike the gods of life and death, whom he neither rivals nor serves, the Pale Flame is _utterly outside_ their dominion. Yet they deplore him: Kelemvor’s clergy burn his sigil in silence; Lathander’s faithful salt the earth where his cults meet. For when the Pale Flame raises the dead, he does not _restore_ them—he _rewrites_ them. Those returned by his arcane fire often awaken as **Hollow Ones**—quiet, cold survivors of death, with some tether to their soul missing, and a vacant place left in its stead.

His warlocks, known as the **Thresherbound**, are seekers of forbidden transition. They do not plead for divine favor. They manipulate thresholds—of life, mind, and mortality—through **arcane power**, not holy writ. They wield the mind-warping abilities common to those bound to Great Old Ones: psychic intrusion, combat intuition, domination of the will, and mastery over thought and fear.

To serve the Pale Flame is not a matter of worship. It is a contract signed in frost and flame, on the liminal edge of existence. He offers no commandments. Only clarity:

> _“Let them return. Let them burn. Let them know the price.”_
